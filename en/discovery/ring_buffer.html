Question: Why does $f(\theta) = 1 - d/N$ fix the mixing angles?

The mixing angles of the Standard Model — the Cabibbo angle and the PMNS-matrix entries $\theta_{23}$ and $\theta_{13}$ — are measured experimentally, but no theory explains "why those values." The 2/9 of the Koide formula is likewise an empirical coincidence without a derivation. The Schwarzschild radius $r_s = 2GM/c^2$ is derived, yet there is no structural account of where the 2 inside it comes from.

In the Banya Framework, $f(\theta) = 1 - d/N$ is the residual capacity of the ring buffer. Subtracting the occupied slots $d$ from the ring size $N$ leaves the ratio that fixes the mixing strength. The key point: $d$ is always one of the integers that emerge from the axioms (2, 3, 4, 7), and $N$ is the ring size (7, 9, 30, 137).
