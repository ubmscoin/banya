Λ·l_p² = α⁵⁷ × e^(21/35) matches the observed value within 0.09%. The 122-digit fine-tuning problem is solved at an error of 0.09%.

The cosmological constant problem (the 10¹²⁰ gap between QFT prediction and observation) is one of the largest unsolved problems in physics. In the Banya Framework, it is derived from the single structure α⁵⁷.

The string-theory landscape relies on the anthropic principle for selecting one vacuum out of 10⁵⁰⁰. The Banya Framework derives the exponent 57 from the 7-dimensional exterior algebra, so the anthropic principle is unnecessary.

If the decomposition of 57 can be other than 21+35+1, the uniqueness weakens. Settled if it is proven that no other decomposition exists, or if the 21/35 ratio is independently verified (H-16).

There is room to make the combinatorial derivation path of 57 more rigorous. A CAS-structural argument for why grades 2+3+0 are selected and grades 1 (7) and 4 (35) are excluded is needed.
