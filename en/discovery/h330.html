H-330: Unruh effect = accelerated observer's system-domain time mapping distortion

$$T_U=\hbar a/(2\pi c k_B)\;\leftrightarrow\;\text{accelerated shift cost warps time mapping}$$

The Unruh effect is derived as a distortion of the system-domain time mapping for an accelerated observer. Acceleration = direction change of shifts on the d-ring, and the Swap cost distorts the mapping.

Banya formula: $T_U = \hbar a/(2\pi c k_B)$. Acceleration $a$ = frequency of shift-direction changes. The system-domain time mapping distortion is proportional to the temperature $T_U$.

Axiomatic basis: Axiom 4 (cost) prescribes the Swap cost of shift-direction changes (acceleration). Axiom 15 (δ = system time) provides the reference. Axiom 10 (observer) defines the distinction between inertial and accelerated observers.

Structural consequence: what is not an empty entity to an inertial observer appears as an empty entity (virtual particle = temperature) to an accelerated observer. Because the system-domain mapping depends on the observer, the definition of "vacuum" differs across observers.

Numerics: at $a = 9.8\;\text{m/s}^2$ (Earth's gravity), $T_U \sim 10^{-20}$ K — below observability. At $a \sim 10^{21}\;\text{m/s}^2$ (strong lasers), $T_U \sim 1$ K — still hard to measure.

Consistency: connected to H-329 (Hawking radiation) via the equivalence principle (acceleration = gravity). Shares the same system-domain mapping-distortion structure as H-324 (gravitational time dilation).

Physical correspondence: Unruh effect (1976). The phenomenon that a uniformly accelerated observer perceives thermal radiation. The flat-spacetime version of Hawking radiation.

In conventional quantum field theory, the Unruh effect arises from a Bogoliubov transformation in Rindler coordinates. In Banya it is the mapping distortion of the accelerated observer between system and domain.

Verification: direct observation of the Unruh effect has not yet been achieved. Indirect evidence: possible depolarization in circular accelerators (the Bell-Leinaas effect). Searches in strong laser fields are underway.
