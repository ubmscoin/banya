This card is a B-grade hypothesis that derives, from the Banya Framework axiom structure, why the graviton's mass is exactly 0. The library body phrased it as "cost accumulation is not the FSM norm," but according to v1.8 physics_map.html gravity is not + crossing cost but rather √3 norm accumulation · juim densification contraction (no ring), so the wording needs to be corrected. The essential insight ("gravity is not a gauge force mediated by a mediator particle") is consistent with v1.8.

v1.8 consistent reinterpretation: in the v1.8 four-force classification gravity lies outside the ring taxonomy — ring-7 strong / ring-30 weak / ring-137 electromagnetic all carry mediator particles (gluon / W·Z / photon) on top of a ring structure. Gravity has no ring at all, so there is no slot for a mediator to occupy. This is the structural ground for the absence of the graviton.

The real reason why m_g = 0: gravity is a geometric phenomenon in which √3 norm contraction is distributed directly with distance ℓ as 1/(4πℓ²) (Axiom 13 proposition), not an exchange force from a mediator shuttling between two FSM norms. No mediator ==> nothing whose mediator mass could even be defined ==> m_g = 0 is not a "value" of a mediator but the expression of mediator absence.

Experimental consistency: GW170817 (the 2017 joint detection of gravitational waves from a neutron-star merger and a short gamma-ray burst) measured the difference between the gravitational-wave speed and the speed of light as |Δv/c| < 10^-15. From this follows the bound |m_g| < 1.2 × 10^-22 eV. This hypothesis's m_g = 0 is consistent with that bound. The hypothesis keeps predicting 0 even under future more-precise measurements — detection of any finite m_g would immediately falsify it.

Gravity's infinite range and the absence of a Yukawa modification (no e^(-r/λ_g) factor) also follow automatically from this hypothesis — without a mediator mass the Compton wavelength is infinite, so the range carries no exponential damping. Only the 1/r² dependence remains (Axiom 13 proposition, 1/(4πℓ²) is natural).

The axiomatic derivation of the spin-2 property is left as a task for a subsequent round. The tensor representation may follow naturally from the Axiom 2 proposition workbench √3 (CAS 3-axis orthogonal), but the quantitative derivation is incomplete. After the wording correction this becomes a Class III (axiomatic verbal description complete) hypothesis and a candidate for D promotion.
