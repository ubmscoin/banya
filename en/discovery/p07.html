P-07 prediction (predictions.html#r6)

The Higgs boson self-coupling constant $\lambda_H$ determines the strength of the Higgs potential vertex. In the Standard Model, $\lambda_H = m_H^2 / (2v^2)$ is back-calculated from the Higgs mass and the vacuum expectation value. The Banya Framework derives $\lambda_H$ directly from the CAS cost structure.

Starting from the Banya formula $\delta^2 = (\text{time} + \text{space})^2 + (\text{observer} + \text{superposition})^2$. The Higgs sector is the mechanism that assigns mass during the Swap stage of CAS. The CAS cost structure (R +1, C +1, S +1, Axiom 4) directly determines the magnitude of the Higgs self-coupling.

The total cost of one CAS cycle is 13 (Axiom 6). Among the four CAS operations, Swap ($\|\sqrt{3}\|$) is the maximum-cost operation, and $y_t \approx 1$ means this reference cost equals 1 (derivation demonstration 2). The CAS FSM 011 norm $\sqrt{2}$ divides the VEV to determine $m_t = v/\sqrt{2}$. The Higgs self-coupling is the result of multiplying this unit cost by the CAS full value, the generation structure, and the Compare factor.

The Higgs boson is a color singlet, yet the Higgs self-coupling is influenced by color degrees of freedom through virtual quark loops. $3^3 = 27$ is not a direct coupling but an indirect contribution through virtual processes.

The experimental value of $\lambda_H$ back-calculated from the Standard Model Higgs mass is $\lambda = m_H^2/(2v^2) = 125.25^2/(2 \times 246.22^2) = 0.12943$ ($m_H = 125.25\;\text{GeV}$ PDG 2024, $v = 246.22\;\text{GeV}$). The error against the Banya Framework prediction $0.12963$ is $0.16\%$.

The Higgs mass is also derived directly: $m_H = v \times \sqrt{2\lambda_H} = v \times \sqrt{7/27}$.

The value $7/54$ is uniquely determined by the CAS structure. 7 is the dimension between CAS phase balls, and 54 is the binary branching of the generation structure 27 via Compare ($\times 2$). No adjustable parameter exists. Right or wrong, this is the only value.
