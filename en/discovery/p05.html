P-05: BAO Substructure

P-05 prediction (predictions.html#r5-bao)

The standard scale of baryon acoustic oscillations (BAO) is 147 Mpc. This is the distance traveled by acoustic waves in the early universe up to the moment of recombination.

In the Banya Framework, CAS has a 7-stage phase space. This 7 subdivides the BAO scale.

The BAO scale 147 Mpc is the sound horizon. Dividing this by the CAS phase-space dimension 7 is the hypothesis that "7 independent degrees of freedom contribute equivalently to the acoustic oscillation". The scale handled by each degree of freedom = 147/7 = 21 Mpc. This is not a spatial division but a mode decomposition.

The prediction is that fine substructure with 21 Mpc spacing exists inside the main 147 Mpc peak. This does not arise in standard cosmology. In the Standard Model there is no reason for the number 7 to enter BAO. It is a unique signature that emerges only from the Banya Framework.

In the Banya Framework, the Compare stage of CAS generates a cost of $\alpha$. This cost grows as the energy approaches the Planck energy.

Standard quantum gravity models predict $\Delta v / c \sim (E/E_P)$ or $(E/E_P)^2$. The Banya Framework multiplies this by $\alpha$, because the Compare cost imparts a fine energy dependence even to the speed of light. The fact that the specific coefficient $\alpha$ attaches is a signature unique to the Banya Framework.
