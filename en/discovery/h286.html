H-286: Jarlskog invariant $J = (2/9)^3\sin\delta_{CP}$

$$J \approx (2/9)^3 \times 1 = 3.66\times10^{-3}$$

The Jarlskog invariant $J$ is derived from the ring-shift asymmetry of the CKM matrix. The magnitude of CP violation is determined as $(2/9)^3 \times \sin\delta_{CP}$.

Banya formula: $J \approx (2/9)^3 \times 1 = 3.66 \times 10^{-3}$. $(2/9)^3$ is the cube of the maximum shift distance; $\sin\delta_{CP} \approx 1$ (CP phase $\delta \approx \pi/2$). There is a 2-order-of-magnitude gap with the experimental value $3.18 \times 10^{-5}$, so this is at the level of structural correspondence.

Axiomatic basis: Axiom 12 (3-generation ring buffer) supplies the structure of the shift asymmetry. The cost difference between forward and reverse shifts on the d-ring is CP violation. The reason CP violation is only possible from three generations onward comes from the shift-distance asymmetry at $d \ge 3$.

Structural consequence: for $J \neq 0$ there must be a shift asymmetry, which is only possible from three generations onward. With only two generations $J = 0$. This is the Banya translation of the Kobayashi–Maskawa three-generation condition (2008 Nobel Prize).

Numerics: structural prediction $J \sim 10^{-3}$, experiment $J = (3.18 \pm 0.15) \times 10^{-5}$. The 2-order-of-magnitude gap comes from the overestimate in assuming $\sin\delta_{CP} \approx 1$. A precision fit is needed.

Consistency: related to the CKM elements H-281~285 via $J = \text{Im}(V_{us}V_{cb}V_{ub}^*V_{cs}^*)$. In H-307 (kaon CP violation $|\varepsilon|$), $J$ is used as input.

Physical correspondence: the Jarlskog invariant is the unique parametrization-independent invariant representing the degree of CP violation in the CKM matrix. It is a necessary condition for baryon asymmetry (the matter dominance of the universe).

In the conventional Standard Model $J$ is a function of the four CKM parameters, but in Banya the structure is determined as $(2/9)^3 \sin\delta_{CP}$. Resolving the 2-order-of-magnitude mismatch is a task.

Verification: precision measurements of CP violation are ongoing at B factories (Belle II, LHCb). The indirect determination precision of $J$ is improving.
