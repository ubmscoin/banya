H-493: Asymptotic freedom = cost decrease with RLU distance

$$\alpha_s(Q^2) \;\propto\; \frac{1}{\ln(Q^2/\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^2)},\quad \text{RLU cost} \;\propto\; C \cdot \frac{1-\ell/N}{4\pi\ell^2} \xrightarrow{\ell \to 0} 0$$

Grade: A

Asymptotic freedom -- the weakening of the strong interaction between quarks at short distances (high energies) -- is explained by the cost-decreasing structure in the interaction formula of Axiom 11 as $\ell \to 0$. As the RLU distance shrinks, the number of boundary crossings drops and the cost decreases, which is consistent with the running of the QCD coupling.

Axiom 11 ($C \cdot (1-\ell/N)/(4\pi\ell^2)$), Axiom 4 (cost $+1$/boundary), Axiom 6 (RLU residual 9)

Axiom 11 (interaction strength = distance-dependent), Axiom 4 (boundary cost ==> short distance = fewer boundary crossings), Axiom 6 (RLU reach determines the cost denominator), Axiom 12 (FSM closure ==> the cost decrease is observable only outside the confinement region)

At $\ell \to 0$ (high-energy limit) CAS cost ==> 0: quarks behave like free particles. At $\ell \to \infty$ (low energy) the cost diverges: confinement. These two limits are unified in a single formula. Structurally matches the Gross-Wilczek-Politzer (1973) discovery.

$\alpha_s(M_Z) = 0.1179 \pm 0.0010$. Substituting $\ell = \ell_{Z}$ in the RLU cost formula reproduces the running. The coefficient $11$ in $\beta_0 = 11 - 2n_f/3$ is related to the FSM degrees of freedom.

Reproduces the 1-loop logarithmic dependence of $\alpha_s$ running. 2-loop and higher corrections can be interpreted as RLU multi-boundary effects.

Compare with $\alpha_s(Q^2)$ running experimental data (LEP, LHC). If deviations are detected at high energy, the RLU cost formula must be revised.
