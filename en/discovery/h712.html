H-712: Magneto-Optical Kerr Effect = cost-propagation asymmetry of lock bits

$$\theta_K \propto M \;\leftrightarrow\; \text{reflected-polarization rotation induced by surface lock-bit magnetization}$$

Grade: C

The Magneto-Optical Kerr Effect (MOKE) is the phenomenon in which the polarization of a CAS cost wave reflected from the surface of a magnetic material rotates in proportion to the magnetization ($M$). It is the reflection-side counterpart of the Faraday effect.

H-710 (Faraday rotation), H-627 (magnetism)

H-710 (magnetism-polarization coupling), H-627 (lock-bit alignment = magnetization), Axiom 4 (polarization asymmetry of reflected CAS cost)

Polar MOKE: $M \perp$ surface. Longitudinal MOKE: $M \parallel$ surface, within plane of incidence. Transverse MOKE: $M \parallel$ surface, orthogonal to plane of incidence. Kerr rotation $\theta_K \sim 0.01°\text{--}1°$. Magnetic-domain imaging.

Fe: $\theta_K \approx 0.6°$ ($633\;\text{nm}$). Co/Pt multilayers: $\theta_K \approx 0.3°$.

Consistent with MOKE measurements.
