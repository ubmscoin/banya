H-509: EMC effect = CAS cost modification inside the nucleus

$$R_{\text{EMC}} = \frac{F_2^A}{A \cdot F_2^N} \neq 1,\quad \text{CAS cost}(A) \neq A \cdot \text{CAS cost}(N)$$

Grade: C

The EMC effect is the phenomenon in which the structure function of a nucleon inside a nucleus differs from that of a free nucleon. In the Banya Framework this is because the nuclear environment modifies the CAS cost. When many FSM-closed regions (nucleons) are densely packed, RLU paths superpose and the cost structure changes. Fermi motion of the nucleons, binding energy, and nucleon swelling are all unified as CAS cost modifications.
