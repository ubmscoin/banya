H-308: D meson mixing $x_D$ from CAS charm sector

$$x_D=\Delta m_D/\Gamma_D\approx 0.0039$$

The D-meson mixing parameter $x_D = \Delta m_D/\Gamma_D$ is derived from GIM suppression of the CAS charm sector. The reason mixing is extremely small in the charm sector is explained from CAS structure.

Banya formula: $x_D = \Delta m_D/\Gamma_D \approx 0.0039$. $\Delta m_D$ is the $D^0 - \bar{D}^0$ mass difference and $\Gamma_D$ is the D-meson decay width. GIM suppression = cancellation mechanism in the CAS filter.

Axiomatic basis: Axiom 4 (cost) prescribes the mixing cost. Axiom 9 (binary classification) supplies the charm-sector index. The GIM mechanism is the near-cancellation of d, s, b quark contributions in CAS Compare.

Structural consequence: $x_D \sim 10^{-3}$ is extremely small compared with $K^0$ ($x_K \sim 1$) or $B_d$ ($x_{B_d} \sim 0.77$). The reason is that GIM cancellation in up-type quark (u, c, t) loops is most effective in the charm sector.

Numerics: prediction $0.0039$, experiment $0.0039 \pm 0.0013$. Matches within error. Measured at LHCb and Belle.

Consistency: together with H-307 (Kaon CP violation) and H-309 ($B_s$ mixing) it forms the neutral-meson mixing series. The CKM elements $|V_{cs}|$, $|V_{cd}|$ are inputs.

Physical correspondence: $D^0 - \bar{D}^0$ mixing. Discovered by BaBar/Belle in 2007. The only neutral-meson mixing in the up-type quark sector, sensitive to new physics.

In the existing Standard Model, D-meson mixing is dominated by long-distance (non-perturbative) effects, so precision calculation is difficult. In Banya, GIM suppression of the CAS charm sector provides a structural explanation.

Verification: precision measurement of $x_D$ and $y_D$ is under way at LHCb Run 3 and Belle II. The CP-violation parameter $|q/p| - 1$ is also being measured.
