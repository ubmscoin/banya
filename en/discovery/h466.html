Core Finding

H-466: Lepton universality = CAS Compare is generation-independent

$$g_e = g_\mu = g_\tau \;\Leftrightarrow\; \text{Compare cost is identical regardless of generation}$$

Grade: B

The coupling strength of the weak force is identical across the three generations electron, muon, tau (lepton universality). CAS Compare is the operation that compares two states, and the cost +1 of Compare itself does not change with the generation of what is being compared.

In $\delta^2 = (t+s)^2 + (o+sp)^2$, Compare incurs one boundary = cost +1 by Axiom 4. This cost is independent of the FSM norm (= mass) of the object that Compare compares. The act of crossing the boundary determines the cost, not the size of the DATA encountered after the crossing.

Axiom 4 (cost = number of boundaries, independent of DATA content) ==> Axiom 2 (Compare = CAS stage 2, structure fixed) ==> Axiom 14 (FSM norm depends on the Swap result, not the Compare cost). What differs per generation is the FSM norm (mass), not the Compare cost (coupling strength).

$R(\tau/\mu) = \Gamma(\tau \to e\bar\nu\nu) / \Gamma(\mu \to e\bar\nu\nu) \times (\text{phase-space correction}) = 1$. A universality violation would mean that the Compare cost depends on the DATA content, so Axiom 4 would have to be revised.

$g_\mu/g_e = 1.0001 \pm 0.0020$ (experiment), $g_\tau/g_\mu = 1.0011 \pm 0.0015$ (experiment). CAS prediction = exactly 1.

Within current experimental precision, universality holds. The $R(D^{(*)})$ anomaly in B-meson decays is a universality-violation candidate but is not confirmed.

In the Standard Model, lepton universality is a structural consequence of the gauge coupling. However, the $R(D^{(*)})$ anomaly hints at BSM possibilities. In the Banya Framework, Axiom 4 (cost = number of boundaries) guarantees universality.

Belle II's precision measurement of $R(D^{(*)})$, LHCb's update on $R(K^{(*)})$, and the FCC-ee precision measurement of $\tau$ decay are key. If a universality violation is confirmed, a DATA-dependent correction term to Axiom 4 will be required.

CAS interpretation in case the $R(D^{(*)})$ anomaly is real. Unified argument for quark-sector universality (CKM unitarity) and lepton universality.
