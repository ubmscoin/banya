D-20: down quark mass: lepton x color correction

$$m_d = m_e\!\left(9 + \frac{3\,\alpha_s}{\pi}\right) = 0.511 \times \left(9 + \frac{3 \times 0.1183}{\pi}\right) = 4.657\;\text{MeV}$$

Error: 0.28% (experimental value $4.67$ MeV)

The down quark is the result of starting from the electron and multiplying by a CAS structural factor. On the path from the lightest lepton to the lightest down-type quark, the square of the color degree of freedom is multiplied in.

Banya Framework starting point: place the electron ($m_e = 0.511$ MeV) on the d-ring. The electron carries no color charge. The down quark does carry color charge. This difference is expressed as the cost of reading the color channel at the CAS Read (R+1) stage.

Norm substitution: 9 = degrees of freedom of the complete descriptor (Axiom 9: CAS 7 + bracket 2). The Read operation (+, proposition of Axiom 2) adds the 1-loop color correction $3\alpha_s/\pi$ to the base cost 9. $\pi$ is the geometric consequence of the CAS 3-axis orthogonality (proposition of Axiom 2) — 3-axis orthogonal ==> 3D ==> sphere ==> $4\pi d^2$. It is not an external mathematical constant (derivation demo 2, stage 6).

Axiom chain: Axiom 1 (4 domain axes) ==> Axiom 3 (d-ring) ==> Axiom 6 (CAS atomicity) ==> Axiom 9 (cost). In the 1st generation, the lepton ==> quark transition is a path on which the entire color degree of freedom switches on at once.

Derivation path: in D-19 (muon ==> strange) the factor was the attenuation $(1-\alpha_s)$, whereas in the 1st generation it is the amplification $9 + 3\alpha_s/\pi$. The lower the generation, the stronger the color coupling, and the color degree of freedom acts multiplicatively inside the workbench.

Numerical: $m_d = 0.511 \times (9 + 3 \times 0.1183/\pi) = 0.511 \times 9.1129 = 4.657$ MeV.

Error: 0.28% against the experimental value 4.67 MeV. Compatible with the latest lattice-QCD result (FLAG 2024).

Physical correspondence: the down quark appears once in the proton (uud) and twice in the neutron (udd). Since $m_d > m_u$, the neutron is heavier than the proton, which is the origin of beta decay and hydrogen stability.

Verification: together with D-19 (muon ==> strange) and D-21 (tau ==> bottom), all three down-type species follow the "lepton × color factor" pattern. The three color factors differ by generation but all derive from $\alpha_s$ or color degree of freedom 3.
