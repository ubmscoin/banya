H-704: Photonic Crystal = CAS Cost Bandgap

$$\omega(k) \;\text{has a bandgap} \;\leftrightarrow\; \text{forbidden band of a periodic CAS cost structure}$$

Grade: B

A photonic crystal is a CAS cost structure with periodically varying refractive index, in which cost-wave propagation in certain frequency bands is forbidden. Photonic bandgap = forbidden band produced by Bragg reflection of CAS cost waves.

Opal-structure bandgap: $\Delta\omega/\omega \sim 5\text{--}20\%$. Silicon inverse opal: complete bandgap.

Consistent with photonic crystal fabrication and measurement.
