H-462: Parity violation = CAS irreversibility as left-right asymmetry

$$\text{CAS: } R \to C \to S \;\text{(irreversible)} \;\Rightarrow\; P\text{-violation (left-only)}$$

Grade: A

CAS proceeds only in the order R==>C==>S and cannot be reversed (Axiom 2). This irreversibility breaks the spatial-inversion (parity) symmetry. In the mirror image the CAS order would have to be reversed, but the framework does not allow it, so only left-handed states couple to the weak force.

In $\delta^2 = (t+s)^2 + (o+sp)^2$, CAS is the sole operation inside the OPERATOR bracket. By Axiom 2, R==>C==>S is irreversible, and in the FSM state transitions 000==>001==>011==>111==>000 time reversal (= stage reversal) is impossible. The spatial-inversion P flips the DATA bracket (t+s), but does not change the CAS direction inside the OPERATOR bracket (o+sp). Therefore, under a P transformation, the action of CAS is asymmetric.

Axiom 2 (CAS irreversible) ==> Axiom 14 (FSM 000==>001==>011==>111==>000, unidirectional) ==> Axiom 7 (Compare true ==> Swap only, Swap ==> Compare disallowed). Because the FSM is unidirectional, a P transformation cannot preserve the CAS action.

The weak force couples only to left-handed particles and not to right-handed ones. This is the origin of the parity violation proposed by Lee-Yang in 1956 and confirmed by Wu's experiment in 1957. In CAS, right-handed = passes only Read (U(1)), left-handed = passes Read+Compare (U(1)$\times$SU(2)).

In $^{60}$Co beta decay, the left-right asymmetry of electron emission is 100% (maximal parity violation). Because CAS irreversibility is absolute, weak-force parity violation must be maximal.

Wu's experiment (1957) and the left-right asymmetry $A_{LR}$ measurements at SLD/LEP are consistent. Experiments confirm that weak-force parity violation is maximal.

In the Standard Model, parity violation is an input: SU(2)$_L$ acts only on left-handed doublets. It does not answer "why only left-handed". In the Banya Framework, CAS irreversibility (R==>C==>S unidirectional) outputs the left-right asymmetry.

Refutation condition: if right-handed particles are found to couple to the weak force, it means there are exceptions to CAS irreversibility. LHC searches for the right-handed W' boson are key.
