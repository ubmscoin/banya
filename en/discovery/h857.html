H-857. Thermoelectric Effect = Thermal-Electric Conversion of CAS Cost

Core Finding

H-857: Thermoelectric Effect = Thermal-Electric Conversion of CAS Cost

$$ZT = \frac{S^2 \sigma T}{\kappa} \;\;\text{(Seebeck coefficient}^2 \times \text{electrical conductivity / thermal conductivity = thermoelectric figure of merit)}$$

Grade: C

The thermoelectric effect is the mutual conversion between the thermal and electrical forms of CAS cost. Seebeck effect: temperature difference ==> voltage (heat ==> electricity). Peltier effect: current ==> temperature difference (electricity ==> heat). The thermoelectric figure of merit $ZT$ determines the conversion efficiency.

Axiom 7 (cost conversion = heat ↔ electricity), Axiom 3 (FSM norm = carrier energy), Axiom 2 (ECS temperature gradient)

Axiom 7: cost converts between thermal and electrical forms. Seebeck: temperature gradient ==> carrier diffusion ==> potential difference. Peltier: current ==> heat absorption/emission at the junction. Axiom 3: the FSM norm sets the thermal energy of carriers. Diffusion of high-energy carriers = Seebeck effect. Axiom 2: thermal conduction in the ECS lattice = phonon contribution.

High $ZT$: high $S$ (Seebeck coefficient), high $\sigma$ (electrical conductivity), low $\kappa$ (thermal conductivity). Nanostructuring: increased phonon scattering ==> reduced $\kappa$. Thermoelectric generator: waste-heat recovery. Thermoelectric cooler: electronic cooling.

Bi₂Te₃: $ZT \sim 1$ (room temperature). PbTe: $ZT \sim 2$ (high temperature). SnSe: $ZT \sim 2.6$. Thermoelectric efficiency: $\sim 10$ – $20\%$ of the Carnot efficiency.

Theoretical $ZT$ calculations agree with measured values within $< 20\%$ (material-dependent).

Seebeck effect, Peltier effect, thermoelectric device, thermal conduction, phonons.

Thermoelectric devices are commercialized. RTGs (radioisotope thermoelectric generators) are used in space exploration.

Structural derivation of the upper bound on $ZT$ from the CAS cost-conversion model.
