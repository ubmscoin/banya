D-75: Neutron-proton mass difference — A-grade

$$m_n - m_p = (m_d - m_u) - \frac{\alpha m_p}{2\pi}(1 + \alpha_s) = 1.291 \;\text{MeV}$$

Error 0.15%. · Grade A-grade

The neutron-proton mass difference is obtained by subtracting the electromagnetic self-energy correction $\alpha m_p/(2\pi)(1+\alpha_s)$ from the quark mass difference $(m_d - m_u)$. Both terms are derived from the CAS cost structure.

Banya Framework starting point: the difference between the neutron (udd) and the proton (uud) is that a single d quark is replaced by a u quark. On the d-ring this corresponds to a CAS Swap(S+1) operation within the same slot.

Norm substitution: the first term $(m_d - m_u)$ is the quark mass difference determined in D-72 and D-18. In the second term $\alpha m_p/(2\pi)(1+\alpha_s)$, $\alpha/(2\pi)$ is the electromagnetic 1-loop (one d-ring revolution), and $(1+\alpha_s)$ is the strong-coupling correction.

Axiom chain: Axiom 3 (d-ring) ==> Axiom 6 (CAS atomicity, Swap) ==> Axiom 9 (cost). The neutron-proton mass difference is determined as the difference between the CAS Swap cost and the electromagnetic self-energy.

Derivation path: D-72 ($m_d$) + D-18 ($m_u$) ==> D-75 ($m_n - m_p$). From quark masses to the nucleon mass difference. The electromagnetic correction produced when juim binds quarks into nucleons depends on the utterance bit state.

Numerics: $m_n - m_p = (4.661 - 2.16) - (1/137.036)(938.272)/(2\pi)(1+0.1183) = 1.291$ MeV.

Error: 0.15% against the experimental value $1.2933$ MeV. A-grade.

Physical correspondence: $m_n - m_p = 1.293$ MeV is a fundamental condition for the existence of life. Because this difference is larger than the electron mass (0.511 MeV), neutron beta decay is possible and hydrogen is stable. If this value differed even slightly, stable atoms would not exist in the universe.
