H-943: Berry phase = solid angle enclosed by a closed path on the unit sphere / 2

$$\gamma_{\text{Berry}} = \frac{\Omega}{2}, \qquad e^{i\gamma} = e^{i\Omega/2}$$

Grade: A (direct structural)

Geometric phase accumulated when a quantum state traces a closed path in parameter space. Discovered by Berry 1984. Derived by direct application of the quaternion + unit-sphere form.

From Axiom 1's quaternion proposition, a quantum state = a point on the unit sphere. A closed path = the accumulation of quaternion rotations. This is the continuous generalization of the (-1)^n phase from Axiom 1's unit-circle form extended to the 3D unit sphere.

Axiom 1 (orthogonal) ==> Axiom 1 unit-circle form (phase = π × crossing count) ==> Axiom 1 quaternion proposition (space = unit sphere) ==> Axiom 4 norm reading (arc accumulation). The area enclosed by a closed path on the unit sphere = the solid angle Ω. Since phase is arc-accumulation, it equals Ω/2 (phase transformation of spherical area).

(-1)^n on the unit circle ==> e^(iΩ/2) on the unit sphere. If the state encloses one full turn on the unit sphere, Ω = 2π ==> phase = e^(iπ) = -1 (the fermion phase emerges automatically). Half a turn = Ω = π ==> e^(iπ/2) = i.

Solid angle enclosed by the closed path ==> phase is exactly Ω/2. Enclosing a magnetic monopole, Ω = 4π ==> phase 2π (a full rotation) ==> Dirac quantization (H-949) follows automatically.

Experimental confirmation: optical Pancharatnam phase (polarization rotation), NMR Berry phase, quantum-arc effects. All agree with Ω/2.

Berry 1984, Aharonov-Bohm 1959, Pancharatnam 1956, geometric phase, holonomy.

In quantum mechanics the Berry phase is derived from the adiabatic theorem + the line integral of the Berry connection. The present hypothesis derives it directly from arc length on the quaternion unit sphere.

Measurement of Berry phase ≠ Ω/2 would refute. Currently every experiment matches Ω/2 exactly.

Quaternion representation of non-Abelian Berry phase (Wilczek-Zee) — accumulation of non-commuting rotations.
