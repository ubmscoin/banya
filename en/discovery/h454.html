H-454. Weak Measurement = Partial Activation of Compare

Core Finding

H-454: Weak measurement = partial activation of Compare

$$\langle A \rangle_w = \frac{\langle \phi | A | \psi \rangle}{\langle \phi | \psi \rangle} \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \text{Compare}(\epsilon \ll 1) : \Pr(\text{Swap}) \propto \epsilon^2$$

Grade: C

In standard (strong) measurement, Compare is fully activated and Swap is executed deterministically. In a weak measurement, the coupling strength $\epsilon$ of Compare is very small, so the Swap probability is a tiny quantity scaling as $\epsilon^2$. Most events stay on the Compare-false branch and superposition is retained. Ensemble-averaging the rare Compare-true events gives the weak value $\langle A \rangle_w$.

Axiom 7 (Compare branching), Axiom 11 (the interaction strength $C$ is tunable).

Axiom 7 (Compare true/false) ==> Axiom 11 (projection strength $C$ = coupling constant) ==> Axiom 2 (CAS is atomic, but the activation level is variable) ==> Axiom 8 (polling ==> ensemble accumulation).

Weak measurement is not the "all-or-nothing" mode of CAS but operation "below the activation threshold". When Compare is only partially activated, Swap is executed only probabilistically, and a single event yields almost no information. However, with pre- and post-selection, the ensemble weak value can exceed the eigenvalue range (amplification effect). This is a conditional statistical bias produced by the partial activation of Compare.

Spin-1/2 weak value: $|\langle \sigma_z \rangle_w| > 1$ is possible (Aharonov 1988). Consistent with optical beam-deflection amplification experiments (Hosten-Kwiat 2008).

Qualitatively consistent with weak-value experiments.

Weak measurement (Aharonov-Albert-Vaidman 1988), weak values, pre- and post-selection.

Matches weak-value amplification experiments. Refutation: an alternative mechanism that reproduces the weak value without a partial Compare.

Quantitative relation between the Compare activation level $\epsilon$ and the coupling constant $C$ (Axiom 11). CAS path-integral expression of the weak value.
