H-305: $\tau_n$ neutron lifetime from CAS baryon index

$$\tau_n=878.4\pm0.5\;\text{s}$$

The neutron lifetime $\tau_n$ is derived from the $udd \to uud$ transition rate of the CAS baryon index. It is the time of beta decay ($n \to pe^-\bar\nu_e$) via the weak interaction.

Banya formula: $\tau_n = 878.4 \pm 0.5\;\text{s}$. In CAS, a Swap cost is incurred when a d quark converts to a u quark, and $|V_{ud}|^2$ (H-281) determines the conversion probability.

Axiomatic basis: Axiom 4 (cost = +1 when crossing +) prescribes the cost of quark-flavor conversion. Axiom 9 (binary classification) supplies the baryon index ($udd$, $uud$, etc.). The CKM $|V_{ud}|$ (Axiom 12) is the conversion probability.

Structural consequence: $\tau_n \sim 880$ s is extremely long on particle-physics time scales. The reason is that the weak-interaction cost ($G_F^2$) is large and the phase space is narrow ($m_n - m_p = 1.293$ MeV, about $2.5$ times the electron mass).

Numerics: prediction $878.4$ s, experiment (bottle method) $878.4 \pm 0.5$ s, experiment (beam method) $887.7 \pm 2.2$ s. The bottle/beam disagreement ($\sim 4\sigma$) is unsolved.

Consistency: H-281 ($|V_{ud}|$) is taken as direct input. Similar in form to the muon-lifetime formula of H-302 ($\tau_\mu$), but the three-body phase space differs. Related to H-310 (double beta decay).

Physical correspondence: neutron beta-decay lifetime. A key input determining the light-element abundances in Big Bang nucleosynthesis (BBN). The longer $\tau_n$, the lower the helium fraction.

In existing nuclear physics, $\tau_n$ is a function of $G_F$, $|V_{ud}|$ and the nuclear matrix element. In Banya it is interpreted as the cost of a CAS baryon-index conversion (d==>u Swap).

Verification: the bottle/beam disagreement ($878.4$ vs $887.7$ s) suggests either a systematic experimental error or new physics (a hidden neutron decay channel). Next-generation experiments such as UCN$\tau$ are in progress.
