Question: Why is the W boson mass 80.4 GeV?

The W boson mass $M_W$ is a core parameter of the electroweak interaction. In the Standard Model it is given by $M_W = M_Z \cos\theta_W$, but neither why this relation itself holds nor why the specific 1-loop-corrected value 80.4 GeV takes that value is explained. Precision measurement of $M_W$ is a key test of the Standard Model's internal self-consistency, and the CDF II 2022 measurement ($80.4335 \pm 0.0094$ GeV) once hinted at a tension with the Standard Model that shook the physics community.

By analogy: the size ratio between two gear wheels (W, Z) is fixed, yet no design principle explains why that particular ratio.
