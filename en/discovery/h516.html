H-516: Exotic hadron = non-standard FSM coupling mode

$$\text{FSM non-standard}:\; q\bar{q}q\bar{q},\; qqqq\bar{q},\; gg,\; ggg,\quad \text{color singlet preserved}$$

Grade: C

Beyond standard $q\bar{q}$ (meson) and $qqq$ (baryon), the remaining hadron states (tetraquark, pentaquark, glueball, hybrids) are non-standard coupling modes that still satisfy the FSM closure condition (color singlet). Axiom 12 requires only the "closed" condition, so there is no restriction on the number of quarks.

Axiom 12 (FSM closure = color singlet), Axiom 14 (FSM norm), H-501 (spectrum)

Axiom 12 (FSM closure condition: any color singlet is allowed ==> $q\bar{q}q\bar{q}$, $ggg$, etc. permitted), Axiom 14 (sum of FSM norms ==> higher mass), Axiom 4 (CAS cost ==> high internal cost = wide width / instability), Axiom 2 (CAS multi-Swap = complex coupling structure)

Tetraquarks ($X(3872)$, $Z_c(3900)$, etc.): $q\bar{q}q\bar{q}$ or molecular $D\bar{D}^*$. Glueballs ($gg$, $ggg$): pure CAS Swap bundles, no quarks. Hybrids: $q\bar{q}g$. All satisfy the FSM closure condition, but their CAS cost is high and they are unstable.

$X(3872)$: $m = 3871.65\;\text{MeV}$, $\Gamma < 1.2\;\text{MeV}$. Lattice glueball prediction: $m(0^{++}) \approx 1.7\;\text{GeV}$. Many tetraquark candidates.

Dozens of exotic candidates discovered (LHCb, Belle, BES III). Debate on internal structure is ongoing.

Exotic hadrons, tetraquarks, pentaquarks (H-515), glueballs, hybrids, $XYZ$ states

A confirmed glueball discovery would support the strong force account (proving the existence of pure CAS Swap bundles). LHCb / Belle II exotic searches.

FSM derivation of the glueball spectrum. Classification system for exotic hadrons. Criteria for distinguishing molecular vs. compact configurations.
