H-341: Attention = observer filter's selective domain opening

$$\text{attention}=\text{observer opens }k\text{ of 4 domain bits};\;k<4$$

(1) Attention is the observer's selective opening of only k filters out of the 4 domains (time, space, observer, superposition), ignoring the remaining (4−k) domains.

(2) Banya formula: attention = observer opens k of 4 domain bits; k < 4. k=4 means full reception, k=1 means single-axis concentration.

(3) By Axiom 10 (observer filter), the observer receives the δ utterance (Axiom 15) but the filter setting passes domain bits selectively. Axiom 1 (4-axis orthogonal) guarantees the independence of domain bits.

(4) Structural consequence: δ utters across all 4 domains (utterance bit 7), but since the observer's filter passes only k bits, the information of the remaining (4−k) bits never reaches the screen. This is the selectivity of attention.

(5) If k=1, only 1 bit out of the 4 domain bits passes through, so information throughput is 1/4 of the whole. If k=2, there are C(4,2)=6 possible selection combinations.

(6) Consistency: the narrowing of processing bandwidth under focused attention, and the phenomenon of inattentional blindness, match the observer filter's k < 4 setting.

(7) Physical correspondence: in quantum mechanics, the choice of observation basis corresponds to attention's domain selection. A measurement apparatus measuring only a specific observable is the same as the observer opening only k domains.

(8) Existing neuroscience theories of attention (spotlight, feature integration) are phenomenological descriptions. The Banya Framework reduces attention to the structural operation of setting a bit mask on the observer filter.

(9) Verification: switching attention incurs a CAS Swap cost. Changing filter settings = Swap, so cost is S+1. The phenomenon of attentional blink (time lag in attention switching) corresponds to this cost.

(10) Remaining task: the boundary conditions of k=0 (no attention) and k=4 (full attention), the CAS cost model of split attention, and the classification of attention types by domain combination are pending.
