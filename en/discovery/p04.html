P-04: dark energy w=-1

P-04 prediction (predictions.html#r4)

The equation of state for dark energy is defined as $w = P / \rho$, where $P$ is pressure and $\rho$ is energy density. $w = -1$ corresponds to the cosmological constant (vacuum energy). If $w$ deviates from $-1$, then something time-varying (quintessence, etc.) is at play.

Within the Banya Framework the identity of dark energy is revealed in the Master Report: it is the COLD region of the LRU cache — the energy of states that no longer participate in active operations but still persist. This coincides with vacuum energy.

Why doesn't LRU COLD change over time? The states in the COLD region are those whose operations have already terminated. A terminated operation cannot be restarted; once CAS completes, it cannot be reversed. Therefore the energy density of COLD remains constant regardless of spacetime expansion. This is why $w = -1$.
