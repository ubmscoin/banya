Core Finding

H-10: color confinement = CAS atomicity

The phenomenon that a quark cannot exist alone but must be bound in pairs (mesons) or triplets (baryons) is color confinement. The CAS atomicity (Axiom 14) is the origin of color confinement. The cost of maintaining a CAS commit (111) is the color-confinement energy.

In δ² = (t+s)² + (o+sp)², CAS is declared as an FSM (Axiom 14), and the state transitions of the FSM are atomic. Read ==> Compare ==> Swap cannot be partially executed and halted. This atomicity forbids the standalone existence of a quark.

Axiom 14 (FSM declaration: the CAS cycle is atomic) ==> Axiom 2 (CAS sole operator) ==> Axiom 7 (write = juim). FSM atomicity means that intermediate states (Read alone, or Read+Compare alone) cannot stably exist. A single quark = a CAS intermediate state = unstable.

Baryon (3 quarks) = a complete CAS cycle (111, H-04). Meson (quark-antiquark) = a pair of open transactions. A single quark = an FSM intermediate state = energetically forbidden. If energy is injected to separate quarks, a new quark-antiquark pair is created and they re-bind (string breaking = start of a new CAS cycle).

The color-confinement energy scale Λ_QCD ≈ 200 MeV. This is the minimum energy at which CAS atomicity is maintained. Below it, the d-ring must remain closed.

Color confinement is confirmed in lattice-QCD simulations. CAS atomicity qualitatively explains the existence of color confinement; a quantitative derivation of Λ_QCD is possible by coupling with H-09 (asymptotic freedom).

In QCD, color confinement is a non-perturbative phenomenon. There is no analytic proof, and it is one of the Clay Millennium Problems. The Banya Framework describes it as the structural proposition of FSM atomicity.

QCD predicts color confinement but has not proved it. The Banya Framework claims that color confinement follows necessarily from the atomicity of the CAS FSM. The direction of proof differs.

Refutation condition: observation of a free single quark would break CAS atomicity. Deconfinement in the QGP (quark-gluon plasma) can be interpreted as thermal release of FSM atomicity at high temperature.

CAS atomicity is ordered (R ==> C ==> S), but among 3 quarks there is no ordering (symmetry). This asymmetry must be resolved. A path must be constructed by which the FSM ordering manifests as cyclic symmetry over the color-charge labels (r, g, b).
