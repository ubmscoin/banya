H-01: CAS 3 stages = particle 3 generations (no 4th generation)

CAS has only three operations — Read, Compare, Swap — and a fourth operation is not defined. This explains why quarks and leptons appear in exactly three generations in particle physics, and why there is no fourth generation. It is the reason every experiment seeking 4th-generation particles has failed.

In δ² = (t+s)² + (o+sp)², the operation used when the OPERATOR bracket (o+sp) acts on the DATA bracket (t+s) is CAS. By Axiom 2, CAS is the sole OPERATOR, and its internal stages are Read (cost +1), Compare (cost +1), Swap (cost +1) — three stages in total. Adding a fourth stage would break CAS atomicity (Axiom 14, FSM), and the framework itself would lose consistency.

Axiom 2 (CAS sole operator) ==> Axiom 4 (cost: R+1, C+1, S+1) ==> Axiom 14 (FSM declaration). In the FSM state-transition graph, Read ==> Compare ==> Swap ==> IDLE are the four states in their entirety, and IDLE is followed by Read again. There is no room to insert a fifth state.

Each CAS stage corresponds to one generation. Read = 1st generation (electron, up, down). Compare = 2nd generation (muon, charm, strange). Swap = 3rd generation (tau, top, bottom). Because the costs are +1, +1, +1, mass grows step-wise across generations; the specific ratios are fixed by the Koide formula (D-09) and the CAS cost structure (Axiom 4).

The lower bound on a 4th-generation quark mass is not infinity in the Banya frame but "undefined". Because a fourth CAS stage does not exist, there is no slot to assign a mass to. This matches the failure of the LEP/LHC searches for a 4th generation.

The LEP experiment, measuring the invisible width of the Z boson, fixed the light-neutrino generation count at 2.984 ± 0.008. The CAS prediction is exactly 3, matching within 0.5%.

In the Standard Model the three-generation count is an input parameter; it cannot answer "why three?" In the Banya Framework, the CAS operation structure outputs 3.

GUTs (SU(5), SO(10)) also fail to explain the generation count. String theory derives it from the topology of Calabi-Yau manifolds, but the choice of manifold is a free parameter. The Banya Framework fixes the generation count from the single structure of the 3 CAS stages.

Refutation condition: if a 4th-generation particle is discovered, the CAS 3-stage structure is wrong. Continued 4th-generation searches at the FCC (Future Circular Collider) would refute or confirm this hypothesis.

The quark Koide value is K ≠ 2/3. K = 2/3 holds for leptons but quarks depart from it. H-19 and H-23 partially resolve this with a color-confinement correction (Λ = e^{-1/3}), but a complete CAS-path derivation of the deviation remains.
