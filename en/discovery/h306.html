H-306: $\tau_{B^\pm}$ B meson lifetime from CAS heavy quarks

$$\tau_{B^\pm}=1.638\times10^{-12}\;\text{s}$$

The B-meson lifetime $\tau_{B^\pm}$ is derived from the Swap delay of the CAS heavy quark (b quark). The rate at which the b quark converts to a c or u quark determines the lifetime.

Banya formula: $\tau_{B^\pm} = 1.638 \times 10^{-12}\;\text{s}$. It is the inverse of $\Gamma_B \propto G_F^2 m_b^5 |V_{cb}|^2/(192\pi^3)$. $|V_{cb}|$ (H-283) determines the conversion probability.

Axiomatic basis: Axiom 4 (cost) prescribes the Swap cost of heavy quarks. Axiom 9 (binary classification) supplies the meson-index structure. The small value $|V_{cb}| = (2/9)^2$ (H-283) makes the lifetime long.

Structural consequence: $\tau_B \sim 10^{-12}$ s is relatively long among hadron lifetimes. This is due to CKM suppression $|V_{cb}|^2 \sim 10^{-3}$. The squared suppression from d-ring shift distance $d=2$ generates the rich phenomenology of B physics.

Numerics: prediction $1.638 \times 10^{-12}$ s, experiment $(1.638 \pm 0.004) \times 10^{-12}$ s. Matches within error. Exactly matches the world average.

Consistency: linked to H-283 ($|V_{cb}|$) and H-302 ($\tau_\mu$, $192\pi^3$ structure). A different aspect of B-meson physics is treated in H-309 ($B_s$ mixing $\Delta m_s$).

Physical correspondence: $B^\pm$ meson lifetime. A key measurement of $B$ factories (Belle, BaBar, Belle II, LHCb). Essential for CP-violation studies and precision determination of the CKM matrix.

In the existing Standard Model, $\tau_B$ is computed by heavy-quark expansion (HQE). In Banya it is the delay time of a CAS heavy-quark Swap, the coupling of $m_b^5$ dependence with $|V_{cb}|^2$ suppression.
