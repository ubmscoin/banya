H-261: $M_W$ nibble crossing cost

$$M_W = v\sin\theta_W(1+\alpha/\pi)/\sqrt{2}=80.32\;\text{GeV}$$

Error 0.07%

The W boson mass $M_W$ is derived as the crossing serialization cost between nibble 0 (the 4-bit domain) and nibble 1 (the 3-bit CAS). Because the two nibbles belong to different brackets, crossing requires traversing a +, and this serialization overhead determines the magnitude of $M_W$.

Banya formula: $M_W = v\sin\theta_W(1+\alpha/\pi)/\sqrt{2}=80.32\;\text{GeV}$. Here $v=246$ GeV is the CAS Complete scale (H-299), $\sin\theta_W$ is the domain-CAS crossing angle, and the $\alpha/\pi$ correction is the one-loop Compare cost.

Axiomatic basis: Axiom 1 (4 domain axes = nibble 0) supplies the 4-bit structure, and Axiom 4 (cost +1 when crossing +) guarantees the existence of a crossing serialization cost. Axiom 2 (CAS 3-stage) fixes the 3 bits of nibble 1.

Structural consequence: if the nibble crossing cost were 0, the W boson would be massless and indistinguishable from the photon. Since cost > 0, W is massive, and the short range of the weak interaction follows from this cost.

Numerics: prediction 80.32 GeV, experiment 80.377 GeV. Error 0.07%. Only the one-loop $\alpha/\pi$ correction is included; adding two-loop and higher corrections ($\alpha^2/\pi^2$) would further reduce the error.

Consistency: satisfies the $M_W/M_Z=\cos\theta_W$ relation with H-262 ($M_Z$). In H-273 (12 gauge bosons), W± corresponds to the cost > 0 path. Consistent with the decay width of H-277 ($\Gamma_W$).

Physical correspondence: the W boson mass is the mediator-particle mass of the weak interaction. Nibble crossing = traversing the bracket boundary between the domain (space, time, matter, charge) and CAS (Read, Compare, Swap).

In the existing Standard Model $M_W$ is generated by the Higgs mechanism, but in Banya the nibble crossing serialization cost plays that role. Since the Higgs $v$ is the CAS Complete value, mass arises not from a mechanism but from the cost structure.

Verification: compared with the CDF II measurement $M_W=80.4335$ GeV, the error is 0.14%. Convergence must be checked when two-loop corrections are included. Dependence on the d-ring size $N$ should also be examined.
