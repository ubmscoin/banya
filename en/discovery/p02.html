P-02: proton lifetime

P-02 prediction (predictions.html#r2)

In the Banya Framework, the three gauge coupling constants ($\alpha$, $\alpha_W$, $\alpha_s$) branch off from a single CAS structure. The energy scale at which they were one before branching is the grand unification scale $M_\text{GUT}$.

The distance from $\alpha$ to $M_\text{GUT}$ is expressed as a power of $\alpha$. Of the 7 CAS self-inductions, the one relevant to gauge coupling is $19/3$. This is the effective dimension that the three gauge groups ($U(1)$, $SU(2)$, $SU(3)$) occupy within the CAS 7-dimensional phase space.

This value lies within the prediction range of standard grand unification theory, $10^{15} \sim 10^{16}\;\text{GeV}$.

The proton decay lifetime is proportional to the fourth power of $M_\text{GUT}$ and inversely proportional to the fifth power of the proton mass.

The current Super-Kamiokande lower bound is $10^{34.4}$ years. The Banya Framework prediction of $10^{36}$ years is above this bound and is therefore not yet excluded. If Hyper-K raises sensitivity into the $10^{35}$-year range, it will become accessible.

$\alpha_\text{GUT} \approx 1/40$ is the median of the standard GUT prediction range ($1/25 \sim 1/45$). The exact value depends on the GUT model, but here it is used only for an order-of-magnitude estimate of the proton lifetime, so the result is not sensitive to it.
