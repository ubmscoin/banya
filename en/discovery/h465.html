H-465: Neutrino oscillation = phase difference between observers

$$P(\nu_\alpha \to \nu_\beta) = \sin^2(2\theta)\,\sin^2\!\left(\frac{\Delta m^2 L}{4E}\right)$$

Grade: B

Neutrino oscillation is the phenomenon in which, when CAS Compare reads multiple observer states, the phase difference between mass eigenstates accumulates. When observer (Axiom 1) Reads the superposition, phase interference occurs between states with different FSM norms (= different masses).

In $\delta^2 = (t+s)^2 + (o+sp)^2$, the multiple states inside the observer+superposition bracket have different FSM norms. When CAS Read reads these states sequentially, the phase rotates on the d-ring 8-bit ring buffer (Axiom 5). A phase difference proportional to the norm difference $\Delta m^2$ accumulates over the propagation distance $L$.

Axiom 1 (observer, superposition orthogonal) ==> Axiom 5 (d-ring 8-bit ring buffer) ==> Axiom 14 (FSM norm = mass eigenvalue) ==> Axiom 11 (multiple projections). On the ring buffer, states with different FSM norms revolve on the same ring while accumulating a phase difference.

Oscillation probability $P \propto \sin^2(\Delta m^2 L / 4E)$. Phase = (FSM-norm difference) $\times$ (propagation distance) / (energy). Flavor eigenstates (electron, muon, tau neutrinos) = states that CAS Compare reads. Mass eigenstates = eigenvalues of the FSM norm. PMNS matrix = the rotation matrix between the two bases.

The mixing angles D-05 ($\sin^2\theta_{12} = 3/\pi^2$) and D-06 ($\sin^2\theta_{23} = 4/7$) reproduce the oscillation probability. Close to maximal atmospheric mixing but not exactly $\pi/4$, because $4/7 \neq 1/2$.

Consistent with all neutrino oscillation data from Super-Kamiokande (atmospheric), SNO (solar), KamLAND (reactor), T2K/NOvA (accelerator).

In the Standard Model, neutrino oscillation arises from the mass-flavor basis mismatch. Why the mixing angles take specific values is not explained. In the Banya Framework, the CAS structure (3/$\pi^2$, 4/7) outputs the mixing angles.

JUNO's ultra-precise measurement of $\theta_{12}$, the $\delta_{CP}$ measurements by DUNE/Hyper-K, and the determination of the $\theta_{23}$ octant are key tests. Precision test of the $\sin^2\theta_{12} = 3/\pi^2$ prediction.
