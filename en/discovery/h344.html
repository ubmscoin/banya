H-344: No-cloning theorem = CAS inaccessibility of δ utterance

$$\delta\notin\text{CAS Read target}\;\Rightarrow\;\text{no copy possible}$$

(1) The no-cloning theorem states that an arbitrary quantum state cannot be fully copied. In the Banya Framework, δ is the unique global flag outside the FSM, so it is not a target of CAS Read.

(2) Banya formula: δ ∉ CAS Read target ⇒ no copy possible. To copy something it must first be read, but δ cannot be read.

(3) By Axiom 15, δ exists outside the FSM. By Axiom 10, the observer filters and receives only the result of the δ utterance — it does not access δ itself directly.

(4) Structural consequence: CAS Read (R+1 cost) only targets DATA inside the FSM. Since δ is not DATA, it is inaccessible at the Read stage, and without Read, Compare and Swap cannot be executed (Axiom 2).

(5) Copy = Read + Swap (read the original + write the copy). If Read is impossible, Swap is also impossible, so the copy cost is infinite.

(6) Consistency: the proof of the quantum no-cloning theorem rests on the linearity of unitary operations. In the Banya Framework, the sequential dependency of CAS (R==>C==>S) plays the role of linearity.

(7) Physical correspondence: quantum teleportation transmits a state while destroying the original. In the Banya Framework, the δ utterance information is recorded into DATA by Swap, but the original δ utterance itself perishes (irreversible).

(8) Classical information is copyable (CAS Read is possible). The reason quantum information cannot be copied is that it contains a δ component. Pure DATA without a δ component can be copied by Read==>Swap.

(9) Verification: constitutes a Banya Framework proof of the no-cloning theorem. Suppose a CAS cycle exists that copies an arbitrary δ utterance ==> it must access δ at the Read stage ==> contradiction with Axiom 15.
