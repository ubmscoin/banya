H-12: h-bar = TOCTOU lock cost

$$\Delta x \cdot \Delta p \geq \frac{\hbar}{2}$$

= Minimum lock cost between Compare and Swap

ℏ (h-bar) = the minimum TOCTOU lock cost between Compare and Swap in CAS. Identical in structure to the TOCTOU (Time-Of-Check to Time-Of-Use) problem of computer science.

In δ² = (t+s)² + (o+sp)², there is a gap between Compare (state verdict, cost +1) and Swap (determined, cost +1). If DATA changes during this gap, CAS atomicity (Axiom 14) is broken. The minimum cost that prevents this is ℏ.

Axiom 5 (TOCTOU lock register) ==> Axiom 4 (cost) ==> Axiom 14 (FSM). The Compare-Swap lock is mandatory to guarantee FSM atomicity. Lock cost = 0 leads to TOCTOU vulnerability: the state at the Compare moment can differ from the state at the Swap moment. The minimum cost must be greater than 0, and that minimum is ℏ/2.

Comparing position precisely (small Δx) spends much of the lock cost on the position side. The lock cost on the momentum side then shrinks (Δp grows). Δx·Δp ≥ ℏ/2 is the conservation law of the total lock cost. The energy-time uncertainty ΔE·Δt ≥ ℏ/2 has the identical structure: the Compare-Swap energy lock.

ℏ = 1.054571817 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s. This value itself is unit-system dependent, so what matters is the fact that ℏ > 0. If ℏ = 0, CAS atomicity is broken and the entire framework loses consistency.

The uncertainty principle is confirmed in every quantum experiment. Double-slit, Stern-Gerlach, quantum tunneling, etc. all follow from ℏ > 0.

In quantum mechanics the uncertainty principle is a physical starting point. There is no explanation for "why ℏ > 0". In the Banya Framework there is a structural reason: it is the TOCTOU lock cost required to guarantee CAS atomicity (FSM).

In the Copenhagen interpretation uncertainty is "a fundamental limit of nature". In the Banya Framework it is "the cost of an operation". The viewpoint differs: not a limit but a cost. As a cost, it is natural.

Refutation condition: if position and momentum can be simultaneously measured with precision smaller than ℏ, that would mean the TOCTOU lock is unnecessary. If sub-Planck structure is observed below the quantum limit, this hypothesis must be revised.

The absolute value of ℏ must be derived from CAS cost structure. Currently only the existence "ℏ > 0" is explained; why it is 1.054 × 10⁻³⁴ remains unsolved. In Planck units ℏ = 1, so this may be a matter of unit choice.
