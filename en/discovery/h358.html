H-358: Render (Swap) minimum cost = Landauer limit kT ln2

$$E_{\text{render,min}}=k_B T\ln 2$$

(1) A single CAS Swap is an irreversible bit erasure, and its minimum energy cost is the Landauer limit k_B T ln 2.

(2) Banya formula: E_render,min = k_B T ln 2. When a Swap overwrites DATA (Axiom 3), the previous value is irreversibly erased, so the minimum energy is released.

(3) By Axiom 4 (cost existence), a cost is incurred at every + crossing. By Axiom 5 (cost conservation), cost does not vanish. The irreversibility of Swap comes from Axiom 3 (screen overwrite).

(4) Structural consequence: at the Swap stage of CAS (S+1 cost), the previous value of DATA is replaced by the new value. The previous value cannot be recovered. The minimum thermodynamic cost of this irreversible erasure is the Landauer limit.

(5) Numerics: k_B = 1.381 × 10⁻²³ J/K. At room temperature T = 300K, k_B T ln 2 = 2.87 × 10⁻²¹ J ≈ 0.018 eV. This is the minimum energy of 1-bit erasure.

(6) Consistency: the Landauer principle was experimentally confirmed in 2012 (Berut et al.). In the Banya Framework, the irreversibility of Swap is the structural ground of this principle.

(7) Physical correspondence: the second law of thermodynamics (entropy increase) is derived from the irreversibility of CAS Swap. At every Swap, at least k_B T ln 2 of energy is released to the environment, so entropy increases.

(8) Read (R+1) and Compare (C+1) also incur cost (v1.2), but they do not change DATA, so they are not irreversible erasures. The Landauer limit applies directly only to Swap.

(9) Verification: set the minimum energy of one CAS Swap to k_B T ln 2, and confirm whether the relation Planck energy E_P = k_B T_P ln 2 holds from there (T_P = Planck temperature).

(10) Remaining task: the cost of simultaneous multi-bit erasure (n × k_B T ln 2?), the CAS correspondence of reversible operations in quantum computing, and the quantum correction term of the Landauer limit are pending.
