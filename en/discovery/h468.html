$$n \to p + e^- + \bar\nu_e,\quad \text{Cost}_{\text{cross}} = +1\;\text{(domain crossing)}$$

Grade: B

Beta decay is the process by which a neutron's down quark turns into an up quark. In CAS terms this is a cross-domain Swap. The quark flavor transformation (d==>u) crosses a domain boundary, so a cost of +1 is incurred, and this cost is distributed as $W$ boson (virtual) ==> electron + antineutrino.

In $\delta^2 = (t+s)^2 + (o+sp)^2$, the down quark (FSM norm $m_d$) and the up quark (FSM norm $m_u$) exist in different domain bit combinations. When CAS Compare compares these two states and returns true, the Swap is executed across the domain boundary. The crossing cost +1 appears as the virtual $W$ boson.

Axiom 4 (cross-domain cost +1) ==> Axiom 2 (CAS Compare==>Swap) ==> Axiom 7 (Compare true==>Swap) ==> Axiom 14 (FSM norm difference = mass difference). Since $m_n - m_p = 1.293$ MeV > $m_e = 0.511$ MeV, Compare returns true ==> Swap is allowed.

Free neutron lifetime $\tau_n \approx 879$ s. Neutrons inside a proton are stable due to binding energy (Compare false ==> Swap not possible). Beta decay rate $\propto G_F^2 |V_{ud}|^2 (m_n - m_p)^5$.

$\tau_n = 879.4 \pm 0.6$ s (bottle measurement). $|V_{ud}| = 0.97373$. $Q = m_n - m_p - m_e = 0.782$ MeV.

The neutron lifetime bottle vs beam measurement discrepancy ($\sim 8$ s) is unsolved. Whether the CAS cost distribution model resolves this discrepancy has not been confirmed.
