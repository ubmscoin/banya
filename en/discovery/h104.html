H-104: $\text{BR}_{\text{lep}}(\tau) = 1/(2 + 3(1+\alpha_s/\pi))$

$$\text{BR}_{\text{lep}}(\tau) = \frac{1}{2 + 3\left(1 + \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi}\right)} = 0.196$$

Grade C. experimental value $0.178$, error 9.8%

Derives the leptonic branching ratio of the tau lepton $\text{BR}_{\text{lep}}(\tau)$ from CAS stage counts and LUT exit counts.

$\text{BR}_{\text{lep}}(\tau) = 1/(2 + 3(1+\alpha_s/\pi))$. $2$ = number of lepton LUT exits (electron, muon). $3$ = CAS 3 stages corresponding to color degrees of freedom. $\alpha_s/\pi$ = 1-loop QCD radiative correction.

Axiom 3 (CAS 3 stages) supplies the color degree of freedom $3$. The LUT structure of Axiom 6 (RLU) supplies lepton exits $2$. D-03 ($\alpha_s$) determines the radiative correction.

When tau decays, the lepton channels number 2 and the hadron channels have effective exits $3(1+\alpha_s/\pi)$. The branching ratio of CAS paths sets the branching ratio.

Computed value $0.196$, experimental value $0.178$. Error $9.8\%$. C-grade; phase-space corrections are not yet included.

D-03 ($\alpha_s$) and Axiom 3 (CAS 3 stages) are the basis. A CAS branching structure similar to H-110 ($R_l$) is shared.

The leptonic branching ratio of the tau is the sum of electron + muon channels. The corresponding decays are $\tau^- \to e^-\bar{\nu}_e\nu_\tau$ and $\tau^- \to \mu^-\bar{\nu}_\mu\nu_\tau$.

The Standard Model computes phase-space integrals and QCD corrections precisely, while the Banya Framework gets the first approximation purely from the ratio of CAS exit counts. The missing phase-space effect inflates the error.

Including the phase-space correction $m_\mu^2/m_\tau^2$ should bring the prediction close to experiment. Belle II tau precision measurements are the cross-verification means.

The CAS structural origin of the phase-space correction must be clarified. Deriving the detailed hadron-channel branching ratios ($\pi\nu$, $K\nu$, etc.) per CAS path remains.
