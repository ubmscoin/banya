The charged-pion lifetime $\tau_{\pi^\pm}$ is derived as the inverse of the render-pipeline period. The time for the pion to decay into muon+neutrino is determined by the render frequency.

Banya formula: $\tau_\pi = 2.603 \times 10^{-8}\;\text{s}$. Render period = $1/\tau_\pi = 3.84 \times 10^7$ Hz. This corresponds to one period of the CAS pipeline (trigger ==> filter ==> update ==> render ==> screen).

Axiomatic basis: Axiom 8 (screen = render output) prescribes the existence of the render pipeline. Axiom 4 (cost) determines the length of the render period. The low pion mass produces a long lifetime (slow render).

Structural consequence: $\tau_\pi \sim 10^{-8}$ s is $10^{15}$ times longer than the strong-interaction time ($\sim 10^{-23}$ s). Because pion decay is a weak interaction, the render period is much longer than for strong interactions.

Numerics: prediction $2.603 \times 10^{-8}$ s, experiment $2.6033 \times 10^{-8}$ s. Error 0.01%. A highly precise match.

Consistency: compared with H-302 ($\tau_\mu = 2.197 \times 10^{-6}$ s) one finds $\tau_\mu/\tau_\pi \approx 84$. The muon lives longer than the pion not because its mass is smaller but because the phase space differs.

Physical correspondence: lifetime for $\pi^\pm \to \mu^\pm \nu_\mu$ decay. A canonical example of a weak-interaction lifetime. Pion physics sits at the intersection of QCD and the weak interaction.

In the existing Standard Model, $\tau_\pi$ is computed as a function of $G_F$, $f_\pi$ (pion decay constant) and the CKM element $|V_{ud}|$. In Banya it acquires a unifying interpretation as a render period.
