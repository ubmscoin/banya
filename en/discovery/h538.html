The Weak Gravity Conjecture (gravity is always weaker than every other force) is the structural inequality stating that the CAS boundary-crossing cost (gauge force) is always greater than the CAS cumulative cost (gravity). The boundary cost is a first-order effect (Axiom 4: $+1$ per boundary) while the cumulative cost is a second-order effect (H-517), so this inequality is axiomatically guaranteed.
