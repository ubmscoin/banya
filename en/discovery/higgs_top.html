Question: Why do the Higgs self-coupling and the Higgs–top mass ratio take these particular values

The Higgs boson was discovered at the LHC in 2012, but the Standard Model offers no explanation for why the Higgs self-coupling $\lambda_H$ takes the value $\sim 0.13$. The Standard Model leaves $\lambda_H$ as a free parameter. The same holds for why the Higgs mass is $m_H \approx 125$ GeV and why its ratio to the top-quark mass $m_t \approx 172$ GeV is $\approx 0.72$. These three values are clearly linked, yet their origin has remained unknown.

By analogy: the room size (Higgs mass), the column thickness (top mass), and the wall thickness (self-coupling) of a building are all fixed, but there is no blueprint explaining why the proportions are what they are.

D-24: Higgs Self-Coupling

$\lambda_H = \dfrac{7}{54} = \dfrac{7}{2 \cdot 3^3} = 0.12963$

Observed value: 0.1293, error: 0.16%

$7$ = CAS complete value, $54 = 2 \times 27 = 2 \times 3^3$. The Higgs self-coupling is determined by a ratio of CAS numbers.

D-25: Higgs Boson Mass

$m_H = v\sqrt{\dfrac{7}{27}} = 125.37\;\text{GeV}$

Observed value: 125.25 GeV, error: 0.10% (0.7$\sigma$)

Derived directly from D-24. Multiplying the vacuum expectation value $v = 246.22$ GeV by $\sqrt{7/27}$ yields the Higgs mass.

D-37: Higgs–Top Mass Ratio

$\dfrac{m_H}{m_t} = \sqrt{\dfrac{14}{27}}$

Derived value: $\sqrt{14/27} = 0.7200$, observed value: $125.25/172.69 = 0.7253$, error: 0.73% (assuming D-24, D-16)

$14 = 2 \times 7$, $27 = 3^3$. The Higgs–top cost ratio is fixed by CAS numbers.

In the CAS cost structure, the complete value $7$ and the lattice volume $3^3 = 27$ are substituted for the physical coupling constants.

$\lambda_H = \dfrac{\text{CAS complete value}}{2 \times \text{lattice volume}} = \dfrac{7}{2 \times 3^3}$
$7$: CAS complete value | $3^3$: volume of the 3-dimensional lattice | $2$: dual-axis cost

Step 4. Domain Transformation

From $\lambda_H = 7/54$, derive the Higgs mass and the Higgs–top ratio.

$\lambda_H = \dfrac{7}{54} = 0.12963$
$m_H = v\sqrt{2\lambda_H} = 246.22 \times \sqrt{\dfrac{7}{27}} = 125.37\;\text{GeV}$

All three values emerge from the CAS numbers $7$ and $27$.
