H-463: CP violation = irreversibility of the CAS R==>C==>S order

$$\text{CAS}^{-1}\;\text{undefined} \;\Rightarrow\; CP \neq \overline{CP},\quad J_{CP} \neq 0$$

Grade: A

The CP transformation swaps a particle with its antiparticle (C) and simultaneously inverts space (P). Because CAS is irreversible (R==>C==>S cannot be undone), the CAS action is not symmetric under a CP transformation either. This is the fundamental origin of CP violation — the 1964 Cronin-Fitch discovery in K mesons and the 2001 confirmation in B mesons both arise from this structure.

In $\delta^2 = (t+s)^2 + (o+sp)^2$, the C transformation corresponds to an exchange of the OPERATOR↔DATA roles, and the P transformation corresponds to a sign flip of the DATA bracket. Because the inverse operation $S \to C \to R$ of CAS is not defined (Axiom 2, irreversible), CAS$^{-1}$ does not exist under the CP transformation, so CP symmetry is broken.

The irreversible cycle of the FSM generates the phase of CP violation.

The CP-violating phase $\delta_{CP} \neq 0$ of the CKM matrix is the phase generated by irreversible transitions between the 3 CAS stages (= 3 generations). The Jarlskog invariant $J_{CP} \approx 3 \times 10^{-5}$ reflects the quantitative size of CAS irreversibility. Up to the 2nd generation, the CP-violating phase can be absorbed, but at the 3rd generation (= CAS stage 3) it inevitably remains.

$J_{CP} = \text{Im}(V_{us}V_{cb}V_{ub}^*V_{cs}^*) \approx 3.18 \times 10^{-5}$. This size must come from the phase volume of the CAS 3-stage irreversible cycle.

The BaBar/Belle B-meson CP-violation measurements, the LHCb $B_s$ CP violation, and the NA48/KTeV $\epsilon'/\epsilon$ all confirm the presence of CP violation. Qualitatively consistent with the CAS-irreversibility hypothesis.

In the Standard Model, CP violation is the complex phase of the CKM matrix. Why the phase is nonzero is not explained. In the Banya Framework, CAS irreversibility forces phase $\neq 0$. With no inverse operation for CAS, CP symmetry cannot be restored.

Refutation condition: if a system with exactly zero CP violation is found, it means CAS irreversibility is not universal. LHCb's precision measurements of charm CP violation and BESIII's $D$-meson studies are key.

Quantitative derivation of $J_{CP} \approx 3 \times 10^{-5}$ from the phase volume of the CAS irreversible cycle. Establishment of a relation between $\alpha$ and $J_{CP}$. Connection between the strong CP problem ($\bar\theta \approx 0$) and the CAS structure.
