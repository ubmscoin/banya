H-274: $\delta$ duty cycle = Swap probability

$$P(\delta=1,\,\text{Swap}) = \frac{1}{1+e^{n_{\text{Swap}}\cdot E_P/(k_BT)}}$$

The probability that CAS completes through Swap when the utterance bit δ = 1 is derived in Fermi-Dirac distribution form. The δ duty cycle determines the Swap probability.

Banya formula: $P(\delta=1, \text{Swap}) = 1/(1 + e^{n_{\text{Swap}} \cdot E_P/(k_BT)})$. $n_{\text{Swap}}$ is the number of CAS stages up to Swap (R+1, C+1, S+1 = 3 stages), and $E_P/(k_BT)$ is the energy-to-temperature ratio.

Axiomatic basis: Axiom 15 (δ = global flag, utterance bit bit 7) fixes the existence of δ. Axiom 4 (cost = +1 upon crossing +) supplies the cost of each CAS stage.

Structural consequence: at high temperature $T$, $E_P/(k_BT)$ becomes small and the Swap probability approaches 1/2. At low temperature, the probability decreases exponentially. This is the origin of fermion statistics.

Numerics: at $T = E_P/k_B$ (Planck temperature), $P = 1/(1+e^3) \approx 0.047$. As $T \to \infty$, $P \to 0.5$. As $T \to 0$, $P \to 0$.

Consistency: in H-275 (FSM $000$ = idle = vacuum energy), the limit $P \to 0$ is the vacuum state. In H-311 (128 complete states), the 64 fermions correspond to states with $k \le 3$.

Physics correspondence: the Fermi-Dirac distribution. The quantum statistic in which fermion occupation probability depends on temperature and energy level. δ duty cycle = fermion occupation probability.

In standard statistical mechanics, the Fermi-Dirac distribution is a consequence of the Pauli exclusion principle, whereas in Banya it arises naturally as δ utterance ==> CAS completion probability. The exclusion principle is not a separate assumption but emerges from CAS structure.
