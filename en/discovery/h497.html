H-497: Isospin symmetry = u-d exchange invariance of CAS Compare

$$\text{CAS Compare}(u, d) = \text{CAS Compare}(d, u),\quad \text{SU}(2)_I \;\text{symmetry}$$

Grade: B

Isospin symmetry (approximate exchange symmetry between proton and neutron) arises because the CAS Compare operation (Axiom 2) yields nearly identical cost when u-quark and d-quark are exchanged. Since $m_u \approx m_d$, the FSM norm difference is small, and the CAS Compare result is approximately invariant under the exchange.

Axiom 2 (CAS Read ==> Compare ==> Swap), Axiom 14 (FSM norm = mass), Axiom 4 (cost $+1$/boundary)

Axiom 2 (CAS Compare = comparison of two states, cost difference under u-d exchange $\propto |m_u - m_d|$), Axiom 14 (FSM norm: $m_u \approx 2.2\;\text{MeV}$, $m_d \approx 4.7\;\text{MeV}$ ==> small difference), Axiom 4 (cost difference $\ll$ strong force scale $\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}$)

Isospin symmetry is not exact and is broken by $m_u \neq m_d$. The breaking magnitude $\sim (m_d - m_u)/\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} \sim 1\%$. This explains the neutron-proton mass difference (H-505) and the pion mass splitting ($m_{\pi^\pm} \neq m_{\pi^0}$).

Isospin breaking $\sim 1\%$. $m_n - m_p = 1.293\;\text{MeV}$. $m_{\pi^\pm} - m_{\pi^0} = 4.59\;\text{MeV}$.

Consistent with nuclear-physics isospin multiplet mass splittings at the $\sim 1\%$ level.
