H-339: Cost = ordering bottleneck; serialization points determine physical constants

$$\text{cost}=\text{serialization overhead};\;\alpha,G,\hbar=f(\text{bottleneck width})$$

Cost = serialization bottleneck, and the width of serialization points determines the physical constants ($\alpha$, $G$, $\hbar$). Cost is incurred each time a + is crossed (Axiom 4).

Banya formula: cost = serialization overhead. Each R==>C==>S CAS transition crosses a + and so costs +1, i.e. R+1, C+1, S+1. Total cost = 3 (the 3 CAS stages).

Axiomatic grounds: Axiom 4 (cost = +1 on crossing +) establishes the existence of cost. Axiom 5 (LUT) supplies the CAS transition rules. Axiom 8 (screen) and Axiom 15 (δ) confirm that δ utterance and observer filtering do not cross +, hence cost 0.

Structural consequence: a key v1.2 correction. Cost is incurred not only at Swap but at every R, C, S. The v1.1 reading of "cost only at Swap" was discarded in v1.2. With this correction, the cost partition of the 12 gauge bosons (H-273) emerges naturally as $4 \times 3 = 12$.

Numerics: number of serialization points = 3 (R, C, S). Multiplied by the 4 domain axes: $4 \times 3 = 12$. This matches the number of Standard Model gauge bosons (8 gluons + $W^\pm$ 2 + $Z$ 1 + $\gamma$ 1 = 12).

Consistency: H-273 (12 gauge bosons = 4 domains × 3 CAS) confirms the cost partition. H-261~263 (boson masses) show that paths with serialization cost > 0 are massive bosons. H-274 (δ duty cycle) confirms δ cost 0.

Physical correspondence: the physical constants ($\alpha = 1/137$, $G$, $\hbar$) are determined by the width of the serialization bottleneck. Narrower bottleneck means higher cost and weaker coupling. The reason $\alpha$ is small = the electromagnetic serialization bottleneck is wide.

In conventional physics the physical constants are "given values" with no explanation of why they take those values. In Banya there is a structural reason — the width of the CAS serialization bottleneck. It provides an answer to "why this value" for the constants.
