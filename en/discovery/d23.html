D-23: CKM CP Phase Precision: QCD Correction

$$\delta_\text{CKM} = \arctan\!\left(\frac{5}{2} + \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi}\right) = 1.19542\;\text{rad}$$

Error: 0.049% (experimental value $1.196$ rad)

The CKM CP phase is the numerical expression of ring-seam asymmetry. When quark generations switch on the d-ring, the asymmetry of the seam appears as CP violation. $\delta_\text{CKM}$ is the magnitude of that asymmetry.

Banya Framework starting point: in H-21 the correction term was $\pi\alpha$ (QED); here it is replaced with $\alpha_s/\pi$ (QCD). Because CKM is the quark mixing matrix, the strong-force correction must be QCD rather than QED. This replacement tightens the precision from 0.54% to 0.049%, a 10-fold improvement.

Norm substitution: $5/2 = (9-4)/2$. 9 = CAS complete-description self-degrees. 4 = Swap self-degrees (4 domain axes). 2 = Compare self-degrees. The ratio of complete description minus Swap, divided by Compare, is the leading term of the CP phase.

Axiom chain: Axiom 1 (4 domain axes) ==> Axiom 3 (d-ring) ==> Axiom 6 (CAS atomicity) ==> Axiom 11 (ring seam). The condition for asymmetry to arise at the ring seam is when the three CAS stages Read (R+1), Compare (C+1), and Swap (S+1) execute irreversibly.

Derivation path: $\arctan(5/2 + \alpha_s/\pi)$. The leading $5/2$ comes from CAS structure, the correction $\alpha_s/\pi$ from QCD 1-loop. With the utterance bit on, once Swap completes irreversibly, this asymmetry is determined.

Numerical: $\delta_\text{CKM} = \arctan(2.5 + 0.1183/\pi) = \arctan(2.5377) = 1.19542$ rad.

Error: 0.049% relative to the experimental value 1.196 rad. Directly comparable with the apex coordinates of the CKM unitarity triangle.

Physical correspondence: the CKM CP phase is one source of matter-antimatter asymmetry. BaBar/Belle precision-measured CP violation in the B-meson system, and that value quantitatively matches the Banya Framework's ring-seam asymmetry.
