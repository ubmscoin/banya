Question: Why α = 1/137? What is the internal structure of the Wyler formula?

The fine-structure constant $\alpha \approx 1/137.036$ is the dimensionless constant that sets the strength of the electromagnetic force. Feynman called this number "the greatest damn mystery in physics." In 1969 Armand Wyler proposed a formula deriving $\alpha$ as a geometric volume ratio, but its internal structure -- why precisely that combination -- was never explained. The Banya Framework shows that the Wyler formula emerges naturally from the CAS domain structure, and that the number 137 is the triangular-number structure of the domain 4 axes.
