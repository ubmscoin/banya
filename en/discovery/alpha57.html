Introduction

Value tier: Tier S

Between the vacuum energy density predicted by quantum field theory and the observed cosmological constant lies a gap of $10^{120}$. This is called "the worst prediction in the history of physics." Quantum field theory says the vacuum must carry an enormous energy, while observation shows a value almost equal to zero. Explaining this 120-digit discrepancy is the cosmological constant problem.

This report reproduces the 120-digit gap with a single formula via the recursive substitution of the Banya Framework.

Note that $57 = 21+35+1$, and the factor itself comes from $21/35$. Everything emerges from 7-dimensional exterior algebra.

Round 1. Precision Check

First, verify that the numbers actually agree.

Computing $\alpha^{57}$

The fine-structure constant is $\alpha = 1/137.035999084$.

$$\alpha^{57} = (1/137.036)^{57} = (7.297 \times 10^{-3})^{57}$$
$$\mathbf{= 1.586 \times 10^{-122}}$$
Multiplying $\alpha$ 57 times yields the $10^{-122}$ scale.

Computing $\Lambda \, l_p^2$

The observed cosmological constant is $\Lambda = 1.1056 \times 10^{-52}\;\text{m}^{-2}$ and the Planck length is $l_p = 1.616 \times 10^{-35}\;\text{m}$.

$$\Lambda \cdot l_p^2 = 1.1056 \times 10^{-52} \times (1.616 \times 10^{-35})^2$$
$$= 1.1056 \times 10^{-52} \times 2.611 \times 10^{-70}$$
$$\mathbf{= 2.888 \times 10^{-122}}$$
The observed values also land on the $10^{-122}$ scale.

Step 3. Constant Substitution -- Exterior Algebra $\Lambda^k(7)$

Build the exterior algebra over the 7-dimensional space. Given 7 basis vectors $e_1, e_2, \ldots, e_7$, the number of $k$-forms that can be constructed by exterior product is the binomial coefficient $\binom{7}{k}$.

Rationale for exclusion: (1) Hodge duality -- in 7 dimensions a $k$-form is dual to a $(7-k)$-form. $k=4$ is the dual of $k=3$ (both 35-dimensional) and $k=5$ is the dual of $k=2$ (both 21-dimensional), so we avoid double counting. $k=6$ is the dual of $k=1$, but $k=1$ is a connection (potential), not a field strength. (2) Gauge structure -- $k=0$ (scalars) carries no directional information, and $k=1$ (vectors) is not invariant under gauge transformations. The only gauge-invariant physical quantities are $F_{\mu\nu} = dA$ (2-form), the C-field (3-form), and the volume form (7-form).

Step 5. Discovery

The physical meaning of 57 is now clear.

  • 2-form (21): gauge fields. The degrees of freedom of the fields mediating the electromagnetic, weak, and strong forces. In the Banya Framework, this is the connection structure CAS uses when comparing and swapping states.
  • 3-form (35): the C-field. The field that determines the topological structure of spacetime in M-theory. It corresponds in the Banya Framework to the mediator of domain transformation.
  • volume form (1): the unique form that fixes the orientation of the full 7-dimensional space. This is precisely the cosmological constant itself.

Why is the cosmological constant so extraordinarily small at $10^{-122}$? Because it is $\alpha$ to the 57th power, and 57 is the total degrees of freedom of gauge fields + C-field + volume form on the 7-dimensional phase space. For every degree of freedom, one factor of $\alpha$ of attenuation is multiplied in.

Why a product and not a sum: the 57 degrees of freedom are mutually independent. The joint probability of independent events is a product, not a sum. In quantum field theory the action enters the path integral as a sum, but here $\alpha^{57}$ is not an action but a cascade attenuation of coupling constants. Each degree of freedom is an independent filter reducing coupling strength by a factor of $\alpha$, so the product is the right operation. It is the same as the probability of getting heads on 57 coin flips being $0.5^{57}$ rather than $0.5 + 0.5 + \cdots$. Each degree of freedom applies an independent attenuation of $\alpha$, giving $\alpha^{57}$.

Dirac's Large Number Hypothesis

In 1937 Dirac observed that the ratio of the age of the universe to the atomic unit of time is related to other large numbers.

$$\left(\frac{t_u}{t_P}\right)^2 = \alpha^{-57}$$
$$t_u = \text{age of the universe},\quad t_P = \text{Planck time}$$
The Banya Framework version of Dirac's large number hypothesis

The square of the age of the universe divided by the Planck time equals $\alpha^{-57}$. This is the time-domain version of $\Lambda \, l_p^2 = \alpha^{57}$. Translated to time scales, the cosmological constant becomes the age of the universe.

Warning: Dirac Time-Dependence Problem

Dirac's large number hypothesis carries an old problem. The age of the universe $t_u$ changes as time flows. If $\Lambda \, l_p^2 = \alpha^{57}$ holds exactly, must $\alpha$ also change when the age of the universe changes?

Current observations say $\alpha$ does not vary with time. This leaves two possibilities.

  1. $\Lambda \, l_p^2 = \alpha^{57}$ is a snapshot at the present epoch. It is an approximation that holds only at a particular epoch of the universe. In that case the new question "why now?" appears.
  2. The exponent 57 itself is a function of time. $N(t)$ varies slowly and is closest to 57 at the present epoch. In that case the time evolution of $\Lambda$ can be predicted.

Either way, this warning must not be ignored. Resolving it is what completes Tier S.
