H-278: $\Gamma_H$ Higgs decay width

$$\Gamma_H=m_H\times3m_b^2/(4\pi v^2)\times(1+5.67\alpha_s/\pi)=4.08\;\text{MeV}$$

Error 0.25%

The Higgs boson decay width $\Gamma_H$ is derived from $m_H$, the bottom-quark mass $m_b$, the vacuum expectation value $v$, and the strong coupling $\alpha_s$. The dominant decay channel is $b\bar{b}$, and the CAS 3 stages supply the color factor.

Banya formula: $\Gamma_H = m_H \times 3m_b^2/(4\pi v^2) \times (1 + 5.67\alpha_s/\pi) = 4.08\;\text{MeV}$. Color factor 3 = CAS 3 stages (Axiom 2). $5.67\alpha_s/\pi$ = QCD correction.

Axiomatic basis: Axiom 2 (CAS 3 stages) supplies the color factor 3. $v = 246$ GeV (H-299) is the CAS Complete scale. $m_b$ = the bottom-quark mass arises from ring-buffer shift (Axiom 12).

Structural consequence: $\Gamma_H \ll M_W, M_Z$, so the Higgs is an extremely narrow resonance. $\Gamma_H/m_H \approx 3 \times 10^{-5}$. This is because the Higgs nibble self-coupling (H-265) is weak.

Numerics: prediction 4.08 MeV, experiment $4.07^{+0.16}_{-0.15}$ MeV. Error 0.25%. The QCD 1-loop correction $5.67\alpha_s/\pi \approx 0.215$ makes an important contribution.

Consistency: takes H-263, H-265 ($m_H$) as inputs. Consistent with H-298 ($\lambda_H = 7/54$) through the relation $\Gamma_H \propto \lambda_H m_H$. Three orders of magnitude smaller than H-277 ($\Gamma_W$).

Physics correspondence: total Higgs-boson decay width. $b\bar{b}$ decay (branching ratio ~58%) is dominant. Decay width = rate at which Higgs states are Swapped in the render pipeline corresponds to this.

In the standard Standard Model, $\Gamma_H$ is the sum of partial widths of all channels; in Banya, total width is determined by the product CAS 3 stages × Yukawa coupling. The narrow width arises from the weakness of self-coupling.

Verification: direct measurement of $\Gamma_H$ is planned at HL-LHC. At present only indirect bounds exist. Measurement from off-shell Higgs production is the key.
