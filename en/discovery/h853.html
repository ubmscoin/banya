GPS is a system that determines the receiver position by measuring the propagation time of CAS cost waves (radio waves) emitted from multiple satellites. For precision, special-relativistic (time dilation) and general-relativistic (gravitational time dilation) corrections are essential.

Axiom 8: special relativity $\Delta t_{\text{SR}} = -7.2\;\mu\text{s/day}$ (satellite velocity ==> time dilation). General relativity $\Delta t_{\text{GR}} = +45.9\;\mu\text{s/day}$ (weaker gravity ==> faster clock). Axiom 5: CAS Compare compares satellite time with receiver time.

Trilateration: minimum 4 satellites. DGPS: differential correction ==> cm-level precision. Without relativistic correction: accumulated error of $\sim 10\;\text{km/day}$. Ionospheric/tropospheric corrections added.

GPS precision: $\sim 1$~$5\;\text{m}$ (civilian). RTK: $\sim 1$~$2\;\text{cm}$. Satellite altitude: $20200\;\text{km}$. Orbital period: $\sim 12\;\text{h}$.

Relativistic-correction theoretical value $+38.7\;\mu\text{s/day}$ matches measurement.
