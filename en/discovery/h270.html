H-270: filter accumulation $N$ times = running coupling $\alpha(N)$

$$\alpha(N) = \frac{\alpha}{1 - \alpha N/(3\pi)}$$

Banya formula: $\alpha(N) = \alpha/(1 - \alpha N/(3\pi))$. The $\alpha N/(3\pi)$ in the denominator is the Compare-false count $N$ × the fine-structure constant $\alpha$ × the CAS-3-stage/$\pi$ phase correction.

Axiomatic basis: Axiom 7 (filter = repeated Compare) supplies the false-accumulation mechanism. Axiom 9 (binomial classification) determines the effective loop count via $C(7,k)$ combinations.

Structural consequence: since Compare-false accumulation changes the coupling constant, $\alpha$ varies with the energy scale (= filter iteration count $N$). It diverges at $N \to 3\pi/\alpha$ (the Landau pole), which is the saturation state where Compare can no longer return false.
