Core Finding

H-82: CKM u-row Hamming distance monotone correspondence

$$|V_{ud}| > |V_{us}| > |V_{ub}|, \quad d_H(u,d) < d_H(u,s) < d_H(u,b)$$

Structural correspondence. CKM magnitude order matches the monotone decrease of Hamming distance.

We show that the magnitudes of the u-row entries of the CKM matrix are in monotone correspondence with Hamming distance. In the Banya formula, a quark transition is the CAS Read==>Compare==>Swap (Axiom 2), and bit comparison is performed at the Compare step. With the bit assignment of H-44 (3-bit octet), the Hamming distance is $d_H = 1$ for $u \to d$, $d_H = 2$ for $u \to s$, and $d_H = 3$ for $u \to b$, increasing monotonically. Structural consequence: the larger the Hamming distance, the higher the juim (juim) cost (Axiom 4, C+1 iteration) at Compare, so the transition probability decreases. The order $|V_{ud}| > |V_{us}| > |V_{ub}|$ exactly matches the bit-distance order. Numerics: $|V_{ud}| \approx 0.974$ ($d_H=1$), $|V_{us}| \approx 0.225$ ($d_H=2$), $|V_{ub}| \approx 0.004$ ($d_H=3$). Exponential decrease as the distance grows. Consistency: provides the structural basis for H-47 ($s_{13}$ CAS derivation). Consistent with D-07 (Cabibbo angle) and D-08 (Wolfenstein A). Physical correspondence: the near-diagonal dominance of the CKM matrix is interpreted as a preference for nearby transitions in bit space. In the existing Standard Model the CKM structure follows from Yukawa couplings, but in Banya it is reduced to a distance function in bit space. Verification: build a quantitative formula of the form $|V_{ij}| \propto \exp(-c \cdot d_H(i,j))$ and test whether the entire CKM matrix can be reproduced. Remaining task: confirm whether the same monotone correspondence holds for the c-row and the t-row. Establish a quantitative bit-transition formula.
