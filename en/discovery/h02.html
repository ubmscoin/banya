H-02: CAS and gauge group correspondence

The hypothesis: the three CAS operations Read, Compare, Swap correspond respectively to the Standard Model gauge group G_SM = U(1) × SU(2) × SU(3). Read = U(1), Compare = SU(2), Swap = SU(3). A systematic mapping exists between the cost structure and the number of generators.

In δ² = (t+s)² + (o+sp)², the CAS in the OPERATOR bracket has three ways of accessing DATA. By Axiom 2, Read only reads DATA (cost +1), Compare compares two states (cost +1), and Swap exchanges states (cost +1). The internal degrees of freedom at each stage differ.

Axiom 2 (CAS sole operator) ==> Axiom 4 (cost) ==> Axiom 9 (complete description DOF = 9). Read references a single state, so its internal DOF is 1 ==> 1 U(1) generator. Compare compares two states, DOF 2 ==> 3 SU(2) generators = 2²−1. Swap exchanges all three colors, DOF 3 ==> 8 SU(3) generators = 3²−1.

The relation between the CAS cost ratio (1, 2, 4) and the gauge-group generator counts (1, 3, 8): generators = n²−1 (n = CAS-stage DOF). CAS itself is not a group (no inverse, no associativity). However, by H-17 (principal-bundle projection), CAS is the total space of a principal bundle and DATA is the base space, so the correspondence is a projection rather than an isomorphism.

The energy dependence of the gauge-coupling-constant ratio g₁ : g₂ : g₃ can be derived from the CAS cost structure. The meeting of the three coupling constants at the unification energy scale corresponds to the three CAS stages collapsing into a single atomic operation (Axiom 14).

The coefficient 3 of D-03 (α_s) comes directly from the Swap = SU(3) correspondence. The tree-level value (4π²−3)/(16π²) of D-02 (sin²θ_W) reflects the SU(2)×U(1) structure.

In the Standard Model, G_SM = U(1)_Y × SU(2)_L × SU(3)_C is an input; it does not explain why this particular group. In the Banya Framework, the CAS Read/Compare/Swap structure outputs the group choice.

GUTs embed G_SM in a larger group (SU(5), SO(10), E₆), but cannot explain why that particular larger group. The Banya Framework starts from the operational structure of CAS, so the group choice has a basis.

Refutation condition: if the CAS-gauge correspondence is correct, there must be no fourth gauge interaction. If a new gauge boson (Z', etc.) is discovered, the correspondence must be revised. Z' search results after LHC Run 3 are the key verification data.

Open work: mathematical precision-making of the projection mapping (not an isomorphism); derivation of a formula that quantitatively reproduces coupling-constant running from the CAS costs (1, 2, 4); explicit statement of the CAS-atomicity-restoration condition at the unification energy.
