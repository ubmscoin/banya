Inventor: Han Hyukjin (bokkamsun@gmail.com)

Question: How are the 3 cost structures (Swap accumulation / crossing Compare-Swap / self-reference serialization) connected?

The Standard Model's 3 coupling constants -- electromagnetic $\alpha$, weak $\sin^2\theta_W$, strong $\alpha_s$ -- are each measured independently. Grand Unified Theory (GUT) predicts they merge into one at high energy, but the precise relations at low energy are unknown. The Banya Framework asserts that the 3 cost structures of CAS (Swap accumulation, crossing Compare-Swap, self-reference serialization) correspond to these 3 coupling constants, and derives concrete numerical relations.

Core Finding

D-28: sin^2 theta_W running formula

$\sin^2\theta_W^{\text{run}} = \dfrac{3}{8} \cdot \dfrac{2}{\pi} \cdot \left(1 - \left(4 + \dfrac{1}{\pi}\right)\alpha\right) = 0.23121$

Observed: $0.23122$, error: 0.005%

CAS running from the GUT value $3/8$. The factor $4 + 1/\pi$ is CAS 4 domains + circular correction.

D-29: GUT energy scale

$M_{\text{GUT}} = M_Z \cdot \alpha^{-19/3}$

$19$ = SM free parameters, $3$ = CAS domains. Within the GUT range.

The grand-unification energy expressed in terms of $\alpha$ and $M_Z$ alone.

D-30: sin^2 theta_W most-compact form

$\sin^2\theta_W = \dfrac{7}{2 + 9\pi} = 0.23122$

Observed: $0.23122$, error: 0.0004%

$7$ = CAS internal states, $9$ = complete description, $2$ = brackets. The most compact form.

D-34: Triangular relation among the three coupling constants

$\dfrac{\alpha_s \cdot \sin^2\theta_W}{\alpha} = \dfrac{15}{4}$

Observed: $3.736$, derived: $15/4 = 3.75$, error: 0.37%

$15 = 3(\text{CAS}) \times 5(9-4)$. Triangular relation among the three cost structures.

Round 1. Deriving the coupling-constant relations from the CAS cost structure

Step 1. Banya formula

$\delta^2 = (\text{time} + \text{space})^2 + (\text{observer} + \text{superposition})^2$

Use the 3 cost paths of the CAS operation: (1) Swap accumulation cost $\to \alpha$, (2) crossing Compare-Swap cost $\to \sin^2\theta_W$, (3) self-reference serialization cost $\to \alpha_s$.

Step 2. Norm substitution

Apply CAS running correction from the GUT reference point $\sin^2\theta_W = 3/8$.

$\sin^2\theta_W^{\text{run}} = \dfrac{3}{8} \cdot \dfrac{2}{\pi} \cdot (1 - c \cdot \alpha)$
$c = 4 + 1/\pi$: CAS 4 domains + circular correction coefficient

Step 3. Constant substitution

Substitute the existing physical constants.

Step 4. Domain transformation

Compute the numerical value of each finding.

D-28: $(3/8)(2/\pi)(1-(4+1/\pi)/137.036) = 0.23121$
D-29: $M_Z \cdot \alpha^{-19/3} = 91.19 \cdot 137.036^{19/3} \approx 10^{16}$ GeV

D-34: $\alpha_s \cdot \sin^2\theta_W / \alpha = 0.1179 \times 0.23122 \times 137.036 = 3.736$
D-28 and D-30 derive the same $\sin^2\theta_W$ via two different paths. D-34 is the ratio of the three constants.
