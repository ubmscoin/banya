$$m_b = m_\tau \cdot \frac{7}{3}\!\left(1 + \frac{2\alpha_s^2}{\pi}\right) = 4183 \;\text{MeV}$$

Error 0.069%. · Grade A-grade

The bottom quark mass is obtained by multiplying tau by the CAS self-induction ratio $7/3$ and then adding the second-order strong-coupling correction $(1+2\alpha_s^2/\pi)$. It is the result of lifting D-21's first-order approximation ($m_b = m_\tau \times 7/3$) to R2 precision.

Banya Framework starting point: place tau ($m_\tau = 1776.86$ MeV) on the d-ring. The transition from a 3rd-generation lepton to a 3rd-generation down-type quark is a path that only adds the color degree of freedom.

Norm substitution: in $7/3$, 7 = the count of CAS internal states (1+2+4 = 7, Axiom 9) — the total number of non-zero states completely describing one CAS instance. 3 = the number of CAS stages (Read, Compare, Swap, Axiom 2). The ratio 7/3 is the count of CAS internal states divided by the number of CAS stages. The Compare operation (T(N)+1, derivation example 2 3-stage) is assigned to bottom (3rd-generation down), and this ratio determines $m_b/m_\tau$. In the 2nd-order correction $2\alpha_s^2/\pi$, 2 is the two CAS Read-Compare stages, and $\alpha_s^2$ is the 2-loop cost of the strong coupling.

Axiom chain: Axiom 1 (4 domain axes) ==> Axiom 3 (d-ring) ==> Axiom 6 (CAS atomicity) ==> Axiom 9 (cost). The key point is that $7/3$ emerges from pure CAS structural constants.

Numerics: $m_b = 1776.86 \times (7/3) \times (1 + 2 \times 0.1183^2/\pi) = 4183$ MeV.

Error: 0.069% against the experimental value $4180 \pm 30$ MeV (PDG R2). A-grade.

Physical correspondence: the bottom quark is the constituent quark of $B$ mesons and $\Upsilon$ mesons. As a 3rd-generation down-type, it shares the generation with tau, and it is central to CP violation and $B$ physics.
