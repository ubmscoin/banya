H-90: Decoherence Time = Reciprocal of Compare-true Accumulation

$$\tau_{\text{dec}} = \frac{1}{\Gamma_{\text{Compare=true}}} = \frac{1}{n_{\text{true}} \cdot \Delta t_{\text{CAS}}}$$

Structural correspondence. Consistent with the quantum-to-classical transition time scale

Decoherence time is defined as the reciprocal of the cumulative frequency of CAS Compare=true. In the Banya formula, Compare (Axiom 2) is the sole operation that judges state. When Compare=true is returned, juim is executed and the state is fixed (written) (Axiom 7). When the environment performs Compare on the system frequently, the accumulation of true accelerates and quantum superposition is extinguished. This is the CAS mechanism of decoherence. Structural consequence: in $\tau_{\text{dec}} = 1/(n_{\text{true}} \cdot \Delta t_{\text{CAS}})$, $n_{\text{true}}$ is the count of Compare=true per unit time and $\Delta t_{\text{CAS}}$ is the duration of a single CAS cycle. It corresponds to the frequency at which the utterance bit (Axiom 15) turns on. Numerics: for a macroscopic system with $n_{\text{true}} \sim 10^{40}$/s, $\tau_{\text{dec}} \sim 10^{-40}$s — instantaneous classicalization. For an isolated quantum system with $n_{\text{true}} \sim 1$/s, $\tau_{\text{dec}} \sim 1$s. Consistency: paired structure with H-91 (quantum Zeno, repeated Compare=false). Compare=true ↔ decoherence, Compare=false ↔ coherence preservation. Physical correspondence: corresponds to environment-induced decoherence (Zurek), where the frequency of environmental interaction determines the rate of coherence loss. In conventional quantum mechanics decoherence is explained via entanglement with the environment, whereas in Banya it reduces to the single mechanism of Compare=true accumulation. Verification: it is experimentally testable whether the coherence time $T_2$ in quantum computing is inversely proportional to environmental Compare frequency.
