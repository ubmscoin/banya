Question: Why Powers of α from the Planck Length to the Hubble Radius

In physics, length scales span from the Planck length ($l_P \approx 1.616 \times 10^{-35}$ m) to the Hubble radius of the observable universe ($R_H \approx 4.4 \times 10^{26}$ m), covering roughly 61 orders of magnitude. That this enormous range forms a 29-rung ladder in powers of the single constant α, with rung spacing of exactly the integer 1, is no coincidence.

A 29-rung ladder structure in powers of α from the Planck length to the Hubble radius. The integer (1) rung spacing means the CAS cost is discrete.

Round 1. Constructing the α Power Ladder

Step 1. Banya Formula

$\delta^2 = (\text{time} + \text{space})^2 + (\text{observer} + \text{superposition})^2$

In the Banya formula, the norm on the space axis determines the length scale. Since α is the cost of one CAS operation, discretizing the space axis by powers of α yields the length ladder.

Step 2. Norm Substitution

Substitute the space-axis length scale with a power of α.

$L_n = l_P \cdot \alpha^{-n}$
$L_n$: length at the n-th rung, $l_P$: Planck length, $\alpha$: fine structure constant, $n$: integer rung index

Step 3. Constant Insertion

Insert the fundamental constants.

l_P = 1.616255 × 10⁻³⁵ m (Planck length)
α = 1/137.035999 (fine structure constant)
R_H ≈ 4.4 × 10²⁶ m (Hubble radius)
n range: 0 ~ 29

Step 4. Domain Transformation

Starting from the Planck length and multiplying by 1/α one rung at a time builds the length ladder.

$n=0$: $l_P \approx 1.6 \times 10^{-35}$ m (Planck length)
$n=1$: $l_P \cdot \alpha^{-1} \approx 2.2 \times 10^{-33}$ m
$\vdots$
$n=29$: $l_P \cdot \alpha^{-29} \approx 4.4 \times 10^{26}$ m (Hubble radius)
Spacing between rungs $\Delta n = 1$. Integer spacing. The entire ladder is built from discrete powers of $\alpha$.
$\frac{R_H}{l_P} \approx \frac{4.4 \times 10^{26}}{1.6 \times 10^{-35}} \approx 2.7 \times 10^{61}$
$\alpha^{-29} = 137.036^{29} \approx 2.7 \times 10^{61}$
Hubble radius / Planck length = $\alpha^{-29}$. Exactly 29 rungs.

Step 5. Discovery

Derived: rung spacing $\Delta n = 1$ (integer)
Measured: identity (integer by definition)
Error: 0%

Powers of α connect the Planck length to the Hubble radius in exactly 29 rungs. The fact that each rung spacing equals the integer 1 means that CAS operation cost is discrete. The hierarchy of cosmic length scales is organized not continuously but as discrete powers of the single constant α.
