A penguin diagram is the process in which quark flavour change occurs through a loop. In CAS this is an internal loop cost. The extra cost incurred as Compare traverses several generations is the penguin amplitude.

In $\delta^2 = (t+s)^2 + (o+sp)^2$, $b \to s\gamma$ is the path in which CAS Compare transitions to the 2nd generation (strange quark) by routing through the 3rd generation (top quark). Traversal cost = $\alpha/(4\pi)$ $\times$ CKM element $\times$ norm function.

Axiom 2 (CAS 3-stage traversal) ==> Axiom 4 (cost +1 per traversal) ==> Axiom 14 (FSM norm = mass) ==> H-472 (GIM orthogonality). The large FSM norm of the top quark breaks the GIM cancellation, so the loop contribution becomes dominant.

$\text{BR}(B \to X_s\gamma) \propto |V_{tb}V_{ts}^*|^2 m_b^5 G_F^2 \alpha / (32\pi^4)$. The loop function $F(x_t)$ reflects how large the top-quark FSM norm is relative to the $W$ boson cost.

$\text{BR}(B \to X_s\gamma) = (3.32 \pm 0.15) \times 10^{-4}$ (theory). Experimental value $(3.49 \pm 0.19) \times 10^{-4}$.
