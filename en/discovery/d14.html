The reason the Koide formula is not exactly 2/3 but deviates from 2/3 by a tiny amount. That deviation is $-15\alpha^3$.

From $\delta^2 = (\text{time}+\text{space})^2 + (\text{observer}+\text{superposition})^2$: each time the CAS 3-stage (Read, Compare, Swap) crosses a +, one $\alpha$ correction enters. Because there are 3 stages, the result is $\alpha^3$.

$\alpha^3$ = the result of one cumulative + crossing cost from each CAS stage. For the coefficient $15 = 3 \times 5$: 3 = number of CAS stages; 5 = complete description DOF (9) − domain axes (4) is conjectured, but a complete explanation remains open.

Axiom 2 (CAS 3-stage) ==> Axiom 4 (cost: R+1, C+1, S+1) ==> D-01 ($\alpha$) ==> D-09 (Koide parameter). The exact Koide formula is $2/3 - 15\alpha^3$, and the deviation being a 3rd power of $\alpha$ is the direct reflection of the CAS 3-stage structure.

Koide ratio = $2/3 - 15\alpha^3$. Since $\alpha^3 \approx 3.88 \times 10^{-7}$, the deviation is $5.82 \times 10^{-6}$. Exactly matches, digit for digit, the Koide ratio computed from measured lepton masses.
